This is my homework, and it's also Elim's homework.

Elim has been in elementary school for a month now. To ensure she is "on time for school," every day her parents have pressured, lectured, and scolded her. Yet even with all this, she still hasn't learned or adjusted. This Monday, we changed our strategy: "let her be." We stopped urging or scolding her and let her take responsibility for being "late." As expected, she's been late twice already.

Do not exasperate your children. This is the teaching of the Bible. How can we wisely guide our children to become independent? Punishment is a very negative form of discipline. I need help.

Moreover, I truly need to learn to "control my anger." I can control my emotions with anyone else, but when it comes to my child, I am "reckless." Lord, help me.

> Fathers, do not provoke your children to anger, but bring them up in the discipline and instruction of the Lord.
> Ephesians 6:4
