Yesterday, I met up with an old colleague who shared some insights about his time at Alibaba. What touched me deeply was the team's 'icebreaking' activities. The [icebreaker](http://zhidao.baidu.com/question/104589072.html) games themselves seem unobjectionable. However, the form they take at Alibaba is astonishingly obscene and crude. 

Female colleagues openly ask male colleagues about the size of their genitals, and male colleagues unabashedly ask female colleagues if they would consider a one-night stand. Situations like these indeed hit the 'goal' of breaking the ice. Colleague relationships seemingly become closer, and the team appears harmonious.

But, it easily reminds me of another group known for its 'close-knit atmosphere and indistinguishable members'—[maggots](http://dict.baidu.com/s?wd=%C7%F9). Isn't that the case? Members rely on each other, merging into one. Yet, the atmosphere is nauseating.

The crude, vulgar icebreaking activities result in a team atmosphere akin to a disgusting latrine. The short-term effect of 'harmony' leads to a long-term lack of 'morality, ethics, and aesthetics.' This is Alibaba's tragedy. The person who first introduced this idea, whether an employee or a boss, deserves to be metaphorically ‘dragged out and punished’.

When it comes to teams, I aspire for an 'orchestra' model. One that is tasteful and elegant. Team members should have their specialties and unique personalities while being able to unify under an overall melody and rhythm. They should not lose their individuality because of the collective, nor should individuality overshadow the whole.
