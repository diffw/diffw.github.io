Are you seeking great things for yourself? Do not seek them! For I will bring disaster on all people, but wherever you go, I will let you escape with your life. This is what the Lord says. (Jeremiah 45:5 NIV)

Lately, I have neither been joyful nor at peace.

Every day, as soon as I open my eyes, I find myself trying to grasp and acquire all kinds of things. Even though some of them are beyond my needs, and some, I don't even understand why I have to have them—just going with the flow, I guess.

These days, I've also faced difficulties brought on by circumstances. Although it's not impossible to get through, they indeed make life cumbersome, unnecessarily adding time and financial costs. After two or three weeks of struggle, I realize that what I'm facing now is persecution.

As I age, health issues have begun to emerge, and my energy is waning. Sometimes I think, as if my whole life hasn't truly accomplished anything, and now I'm already middle-aged. Is there still a chance to do something?

All these things have made my life and spirit gloomy recently.

Today's scripture, "but wherever you go, I will let you escape with your life," serves as a reminder and an encouragement to me.

Even if I do not obtain the things I hope (or wish) for, even if I suffer loss for standing up for what's right, even if my body is gradually failing, and even if I achieve nothing significant, yet the most important thing in life—God has already promised and given to me, which is eternal life in Jesus Christ.

Unlike others, I already know my final destination, and that destination is complete, beautiful, and glorious. Therefore, I ought to live without worries.
