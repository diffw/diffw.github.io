Jan 26, 2021 at 6:09 AM

30 "You of little faith! The grass of the field is here today and tomorrow is thrown into the fire, yet God clothes it in such splendor. How much more will He clothe you?" (Matthew 6:30 NIV)

"The wildflowers of the field are temporary today and tomorrow thrown into the oven, yet God dresses them in such a way. How much more will He do for you, O you of little faith!" (Wen-Li Holy Bible Translation)

Brother Oswald Chambers mentioned the word "simplicity"—a life that is simple and pure. I often strive for simplicity in design, yet I've never considered simplicity on a life level. Is my life simple and pure?

I think not. My life, heart, and mind are filled with various desires, worries, pressures, judgments, dissatisfaction, concerns... simply put, "cluttered." Like a landfill, it's chaotic and piled high.

I always envy children around four or five years old; their lives are so simple, carefree. A piece of paper or a leaf can make them incredibly happy, reaching a state of "pure happiness." Their lives are simple, pure, and clear.

Jesus said, "Truly I tell you, unless you change and become like little children, you will never enter the kingdom of heaven." Like Nicodemus, I also want to ask Jesus, "Teacher, I am now middle-aged, how can I become like a little child?"

Today, Lord, I ask you to help me, guide me, to "turn back" to You. I want to entrust all my worries and concerns to You. Please help clean and purify the room of my heart so that my life gradually becomes simple.

Why in work (design), family life, or interpersonal relationships do I arrogantly presume myself wiser and more capable than Jesus Christ? May God grant me the faith to rely simply on Him.
