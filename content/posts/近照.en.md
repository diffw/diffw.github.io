I haven't shown my face in a while. In order to prepare a team member introduction, I took out the 400D to photograph everyone. I put a lot of thought into the layout design, which allowed me to apply some of the theories I've been reading about in [‘Layout Design’](http://book.douban.com/subject/2238320/). (I recommend it to designers interested in layout design.)

I don't know how far this team can go, but at least for as long as I'm here, I want to integrate design into every aspect of the company so that my colleagues can feel the presence of design. Thus, even when preparing a member introduction, previously "having everything necessary" might have sufficed, but now, I strive for excellence. If we're fortunate enough to become a full-fledged company, I hope it's one truly driven by design. Mere slogans are insufficient; we must take real actions. At the very least, we need to convey the essence of design to our teammates through actions and details.

Today, for instance, Hou Ye, who is our software engineer, remarked, "With this approach, the resume looks a lot more high-end."

This is where the sense of accomplishment lies.
