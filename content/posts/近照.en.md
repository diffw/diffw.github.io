It's been a while since I've shown my face. To prep for a team introduction, I took out the 400D and snapped some photos of everyone. Spent quite a bit of effort on the layout design, using some theories from the book ["Layout Design"](http://book.douban.com/subject/2238320/) I've been reading lately. (I recommend it to designers interested in layout design.)

Not sure how far this team will go, but for as long as I'm here, I want to infuse design into every aspect of the company. So even for a team introduction—which in the past might have been just "everything it should be"—I now aim to make it outstanding. If we're fortunate enough to become a company, I hope it's genuinely design-driven. Just shouting slogans isn't enough; we need to make it real. At the very least, through actions and details, I want my teammates to feel the presence of design.

Just today, Houye, a software engineer, said, "Creating a resume this way gives it a much classier feel."

There's a sense of achievement in that.
