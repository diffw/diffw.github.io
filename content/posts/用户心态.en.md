Becoming more and more like a gourmet, tasting different products, casually chewing on them, and then reaching a "professional" conclusion. There are so many delicious things in the world, but if it's just for the sake of tasting itself, or just to achieve the goal of "having tasted this," the result is that, inadvertently, I no longer understand how to enjoy it.

Whether it's Instagram, read-it-later services, or Path, we consume them one by one, but are we really enjoying them? Are we truly using them with the mindset of an ordinary person? If we constantly adopt the posture of a "critic," we grow further and further away from being a "user." That phrase so often on designers' lips, "I think the user is like this...", has become meaningless, because you're really not standing in the user's shoes.

The Bible says, guard your heart above all else. Indeed, it is so.

Knowing restraint is truly valuable. :)
