Jan 29, 2021 at 6:30 AM

I asked, 'Who are you, Lord?' The Lord replied, 'I am Jesus, whom you are persecuting.' (Acts 26:15 NIV)

Many people ask me why I left my previous job (Liulishuo)? Why did I choose my current lifestyle and work (work style)? Each time, I try to answer honestly, sharing the main reasons behind my decisions.

Although I deeply and carefully considered these major decisions, limited by human ignorance of the future and my own folly and limitations, upon later reflection, those reasons seem incomplete and very superficial.

Often, I wonder how I made such decisions (leaving my hometown of Changsha for Hangzhou alone in 2006, getting married quickly in 2009) or marvel at how I managed to make the "right" choices (from a personal interest standpoint, like leaving Liulishuo in 2019 just months before the stock price plummeted).

Every time I review these moments, I feel a force guiding me, even pushing and compelling me. I believe this is God's guidance. Therefore, I thank God.

My understanding of God has always been retrospective. So, God seems to be a God who "lives in my memories," a God who was "with me in the past." Now, and in the future, is God with me? "Who are you, Lord?"

Regarding "understanding God's will" in personal direction and life matters, five or six years ago, I desperately sought a supernatural experience of hearing God's voice but found none; in the following one or two years, disappointment turned into numbness and indifference, and I stopped seeking. Eventually, I chose to return to the word of the Bible to understand God's will and guidance for me.

Returning to the Bible to understand God's will, I think, cannot be wrong. Yet, it seems to lack a sense of vibrant liveliness. Today, Brother Chambers reminded me that God speaks to me through the situations, people, and events I encounter. Does this environment and work make me more like Jesus, with a gentle and humble spirit, helping me grow? Rather than leading to self-satisfaction and pride.

Gratitude:

1. For the first time, I spoke on design philosophy at WD. Initially nervous, but after silent prayer, I became calm and relaxed. Thank God for His protection;
2. Yesterday evening, I drove alone to Sam's Club to shop for the New Year. I enjoy driving, feeling relaxed and free. It was a chance to clear my mind, as I hadn't been out for several days;
3. Thankful for a phone conversation with Dayu. Conversing with family, friends, and brothers and sisters, even though it provides no tangible help, warms the heart and conveys love and being loved; words hold power. Because God, who created us in His image, created the heavens and the earth with His words.
