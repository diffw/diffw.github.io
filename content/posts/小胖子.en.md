Busy life finally slowed down; if I stayed that busy, even Yanbing would have an opinion.

Saturday was a perfect autumn day. Some friends from church and I gathered at the Hangzhou Botanical Garden. Hangzhou is full of scenic spots—if you don't know how to appreciate and enjoy them, it's really a waste.

Everyone was relaxed, with plenty of snacks and drinks. After filling up, a few of the guys went to the water's edge to catch crayfish.

After the group activity, the teacher felt it wasn't enough, and the teacher's wife said, "We haven't played enough," so we went to play pool.

In the land of the blind, the one-eyed man is king. Among my college friends, I used to be terrible at pool; now, I'm a pro.

But my luck was really good—I actually won from start to finish. Plus, I enjoy making powerful shots.

Haha, my little baby is so adorable now and can interact with us. Every time I press lightly on the spot where the baby is in Yanbing's belly and let go, after two or three seconds, the baby responds with a little jump. It's so interesting.
