Where I am going, you cannot follow now, but you will follow later. (John 13:36)

Every Christian is attracted to Jesus (or Jesus's church) at a unique moment, led back home by the Lord. The first following of Jesus is relatively superficial, yet extremely important. I first went to church because someone invited me to sing and eat on a Sunday; I mistakenly thought it was karaoke and a meal. Once there, I was drawn by the hymns and the love among brothers and sisters.

I reflect on what my "three rooster crowing" moment is. It's a person's rock bottom, a sudden awakening to the true self: loveless, powerless, and without reliance.

Looking back, there were many moments when I despaired of myself and turned to God, such as harboring resentment to the point of unforgiveness, moments of existential despair and emptiness, repeatedly being defeated by sins like lust, greed, and anger without any resistance. At these times, thanks to the guidance of the Holy Spirit, I truly "followed Jesus," saw myself as nothing, and relied on grace.

Yet, for many years, my life's state has been a rollercoaster. I've pondered (even recently) whether a major event is necessary to drive me to an absolute low, where I can completely deny myself, stop relying on myself, and submit before God to follow Jesus. I believe that in faith, I need to encounter greater despair in myself (and likely will), but I should not expect that through a single event, I will become completely holy. After Peter went through the event of the rooster crowing three times, he followed Jesus again, yet he did not become completely holy and still had many imperfections thereafter. As humans, we are never perfect (but those who believe in Jesus are no longer regarded as sinners but as righteous).

As Chambers said, no matter how much Jesus has changed my life, never rely solely on those changes, but rely on the Holy Spirit alone. Every day, put on the Lord Jesus.

You cannot follow me now, but you will follow later. Jesus's words bring me hope, hope in that eternal kingdom.
