Feb 1, 2021 at 6:22 AM

"For Christ did not send me to baptize, but to preach the gospel—not with wisdom and eloquence, lest the cross of Christ be emptied of its power." (1 Corinthians 1:17 NIV)

I've spent too much time on my personal "salvation and sanctification," especially "sanctification" (becoming holy like Christ). Clearly, personal salvation and sanctification are fundamental and important. However, they're not the ultimate purpose or mission in life. Salvation and sanctification (apparently) are processes of renewing and equipping oneself to become a "vessel fit for use" with the aim of glorifying God, exalting Christ, and making disciples of all nations.

Brother Chambers' reminder made me realize that I was overly "obsessed" with my own "sanctification," to the point of "ignoring the world outside my window" and "caring only for myself." My daily prayers and requests were all about personal and family matters. This too had become "idolatry."

The gospel of Jesus Christ is powerful, and my life has been transformed by it. Yet now, I'm restraining this power in myself. I have truly benefited from the "gospel of Jesus Christ," but why don't I have the fervor to share these benefits with those around me? Although I've been sharing the gospel with colleagues and friends over the years, I know it's far from enough. There are still many relatives, friends, and coworkers to whom I've never preached the gospel, let alone strangers.

Paul says, "to preach the gospel—not with wisdom and eloquence." This scripture gives me great encouragement. I don't need to worry about the limitations of my knowledge, wisdom, or eloquence; just preach. The ability to make people listen and accept the gospel does not lie with me; Jesus Christ will naturally accomplish His work.

It seems like another "armchair strategizing" session with no action this morning. However, this kind of strategizing is very important. Because in this process of devotion and reflection, my understanding and views are gradually being transformed. Action stems from the change in perspective.

Thanksgiving:

1. Thank God for His protection, allowing me to restart my 5:30 AM devotions. On Saturday and Sunday mornings, laziness and slackness tempted me to escape from the "heavy" devotional life with God and return to the world for "rest," so I paused. But in truth, neither my body nor soul felt relaxed or rested. From now on, even on Saturdays and Sundays, there will be no "resting" but continuous devotions;
2. Thank YT for the Sunday sermon. In the most important relationships in life—marriage, parents and children, workplace (leaders, coworkers)—we need to update to heavenly values and understanding, putting to death earthly life. Especially at work, we must put to death the "hired hand" mentality; work is not about fulfilling personal ambitions, nor is it about realizing one's value, but rather about displaying God's glory. For human work is an imitation of God's creation and management;
3. Thank the young brothers and sisters for their care for our family and planning to help take care of the child for a day, giving my spouse and me time to be alone and communicate;
