Over the past two weeks, I've been organizing the company's products. Sometimes everything is clear, sometimes I find myself utterly perplexed. There's so much that needs to change and improve. But the overall situation is what it is, and my personal thoughts and actions feel so weak in the face of it. A question often arises: Do I really have the power to change anything?

This morning I saw a [year-end special](http://news.163.com/special/0001125G/2009ending.html) by NetEase that deeply moved me. It suddenly struck me: our country is like a failing company—decayed, aging, unfair, promoting a corporate culture of "interest", focusing everything on money, and so on. In this company, there are many employees whose efforts don't match their rewards, many who feel dissatisfied, many who complain. There are also many who think as long as they get their paycheck at the end of the month, that's enough. Some employees have great ideas, and some are actively doing things. But this dire situation is too big, so big that when an individual stands up to try to do something, they feel immense pressure, so big that employees with ideas eventually think about giving up.

But who will do these things?

Someone has to do it. Whether it's a company or a country.
