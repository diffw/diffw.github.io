After graduating from university in 2005, I've been working for nearly 14 years, spending almost 12 of them in design. In recent years, I've shifted from professional design to design management. In 2018, I transitioned to business and product roles (which, in retrospect, was borderline passable).

I've found myself a bit perplexed about design. Perhaps this is an age where confusion is to be expected.

I need some time to reflect, have discussions with mentors and friends. One day, some questions suddenly came to my mind: Why did I choose design in the beginning? What moments in my design career brought me passion and achievement?

I attempted to look back.

### Why Did I Choose Design in the First Place?

I started learning art in middle school. In high school, I heard the term "graphic decoration" from my middle school art teacher's husband, Mr. Chen Jingyu. However, during high school, I knew nothing about a career in design. Choosing graphic design in university was basically a shot in the dark.

In university, I tried to learn traditional design basics like the "three fundamentals of composition." But what truly attracted me was web design. Two people opened the door for me: the first was my high school roommate, Chen Xinrong, who used a tool called FrontPage to create a personal homepage accessible online (later he used Dreamweaver); the second was my younger brother, who was in vocational school learning web design. Back then, I simply thought web design was pretty cool.

Subsequently, I got somewhat obsessed with personal homepage design. Before sophomore year, I was addicted to gaming. During the summer break of my sophomore year, I stayed in my dorm and, through CDs, learned Dreamweaver and Flash (I had learned Photoshop before sophomore year). Thus began my more than a decade-long "design & creation of personal homepages" journey, which continues to this day.

In the second semester of my junior year, I was already freelancing as a web designer for companies outside school, while also holding a steady design gig at the university's news website. Earning money through my hobby was a fun thing.

To sum up, I entered design purely out of interest and a series of coincidences.

Then, from 2005 to 2009, I spent four years in web design. Around the early development of mobile internet in 2009, I switched to App design. This choice was motivated by both interest and a simple assessment of trends.

Since then, for the past 10 years, I've primarily focused on App design.

### Passion & Achievement Moments in Design

- During university, I played around with creating various versions of personal homepages (which was part of the internet design culture of that era), purely for interest and hobby (before graduation, Pang Dongsheng from 51.com invited me to join).
- Basic self-sufficiency through on-campus and off-campus web design gigs brought a sense of accomplishment.
- In 2010-2011, I participated as an early designer (interaction & visual) in the design of the first Aliyun Phone. It was a very open design environment.
- From 2011-2013, the design standards for iOS apps in the industry improved and their styles became more diverse. I was very keen on experiencing new apps, studying, and mimicking new interaction methods.
- Around September 2012, dissatisfied with the Sina Weibo experience, I designed a Chrome extension to improve it—Weibo Quick & Simple. The extension gained over 100,000 users in a short time. It was a personally-driven tool designed entirely based on my preferences, and receiving user praise gave me immense satisfaction.
- At the end of 2012, I met Wang Yi and experienced the early prototype of Liulishuo. I was captivated by Liulishuo's core interaction (red and green grading, human-machine dialogue). I redesigned the Liulishuo App in a way that aligned with my usage habits and aesthetics purely out of interest. Although it later achieved one million and ten million users, and eventually the company went public, the most enjoyable part was designing the first version (even though it was overly simplistic and quite user-unfriendly).
- In the subsequent years at Liulishuo, I gradually shifted from product-related design to team design and process design, transitioning from individual projects to high-quality team output. Milestones worth mentioning include the first official version of Dong Ni English and the IELTS mock test experience at Liulishuo.
- Engaging improvisational projects at Liulishuo, such as the "The World Is Big" series of products, the first Hackweek, and three brand design proposals were particularly enjoyable.
- Around 2014, I created a tiny t-shirt brand called "Light and Salt Life," which was basically spending money on a hobby, but it was fun. Additionally, small scale design works for friends during limited free time were enjoyable too: a brochure for Nanjing EIS School, the MGC brand, and the L&W wedding invitation.

This is a tentative reflection.
