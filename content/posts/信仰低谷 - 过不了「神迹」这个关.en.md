I am currently going through a valley phase in my faith.

When I need to pray for God's powerful healing and change for my brothers and sisters who are seriously ill or in distress, I have no confidence at all. Miracles have always been a part of my understanding of God that I cannot get past. It was like this since Weiwei passed away from cancer in 2012.

I have begun to doubt my faith. What exactly do I believe in?

I examine my faith from the aspects of creation, fall, and redemption.

**Creation.** With what I see—the mountains, rivers, lakes, seas, flowers, trees, various animals, and my two children growing up—I believe in God's creation. For the vast universe and endless microscopic world that my eyes cannot see, I believe by faith that these are also God's creation.

**Fall.** The greatest sins I see in myself are lust and pride. Before believing in Christ, these were unknown to others, and I never thought they were sins or had anything wrong with them. After believing in Christ, I increasingly feel the filth of these sins and detest them in my heart, but I cannot overcome them on my own. This conflict between spirit and flesh is what is described in the Bible. Or rather, the spiritual battle described in the Bible is exactly what I experience. I must acknowledge the truth of the Bible.

**Redemption.** I have never seen God with my own eyes or heard His voice with my own ears. However, through prayer (sometimes it seems like praying to the air), I have experienced the freedom of sin forgiven.

**Resurrection.** I am someone who is afraid of death, fearful of dead bodies, and terrified of dying. Yet, the Christian faith gives me immense peace and a sense of security—I have no "worries" about the future and no doubts about my ultimate destiny. Sometimes, I even long to leave this world early.

It's just that whenever I encounter the issue of "miracles," I cannot get past it. To put it bluntly, I believe in the myriad of things in the world, those that are described as "natural," and even healing through medication—all these are God's work, these are miracles, traces of God's work. Yet, what we often ask for in miracles are the supernatural. If we understand natural creation and laws as miracles, then the miracles we hope for are for God to break His own work and intervene in it. These kinds of miracles indeed appear in the Bible, and indeed, because of people's prayers, God performed such miracles. I believe "In the beginning, God created the heavens and the earth," and I also believe God has "supernatural" miracles, but my faith only goes as far as praying "may Your will be done as a miracle." I lack the faith to pray for miracles to be realized.
