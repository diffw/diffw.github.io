I decided to spend 10 minutes writing this blog.

Last week, I went to Suzhou to meet my girlfriend's parents for the first time. I was super nervous, trying to act calm. Haha. It felt a bit like a negotiation.

I come with nothing but hope that they would allow their daughter to marry me. I shared everything honestly—I have no house, no car, and the house back home is in poor condition. Perhaps years later, I still won’t be able to afford a house. I also told them I plan to start a business, aiming to do something small.

Her family laid out some expectations for me. They simply hope their daughter can have a stable life, a harmonious family, and good relations with both me and my family.

In the middle of dinner, her mother and older brother stepped out for a moment. At that time, her usually reserved father seriously advised that, from a long-term perspective, renting in Hangzhou might be more suitable. He even compared buying and renting for me. I was very touched by his thoughtfulness.

Her mother is reluctant to let her daughter marry far away in Changsha. It’s an issue. I’m also unsure when I'll return to Changsha.

In the evening, her older brother “threatened” me. Considering he's technically a half-elder, being 46, he told me he used to be a troublemaker in his youth, solving everything with fights; three or four people couldn’t get near him. His grandfather was a Kuomintang military officer with over 80 disciples; their village has 200-300 people from the Kang family. Then he told me not to let his sister suffer any unprovoked grievances. With leopard eyes, he gave me a meaningful look. Haha, it was practically intimidation.

I know how to cherish what I have.

Despite the nerves and exhaustion, the past few days have been very happy.

That's it for meeting the parents.

Another matter: I’m on annual leave this week.

Regarding work, I really appreciate my colleagues a lot. I’m also grateful to my boss. Everyone is great to work with and has offered me much help, sharing their breakfast with me when I forgot mine and being very accommodating.

Special thanks to Dongdong the "eccentric" for his support and understanding at work.

Lately, I’ve been increasingly unclear about how to approach design work. It’s been suffocating and frustrating, not knowing where to start. I've made no progress on a logo for a leaderboard after a week.

Originally, I planned to take a vacation after the New Year, but I decided to use these 5 days to think things over.

A lot of ideas need sorting out, some need summarizing. I also need to practice some visual and creative work.

I hope these 5 days will be fruitful.

I’ll return to Changsha on the 24th and hope to meet some friends then.

Happy New Year, everyone.
