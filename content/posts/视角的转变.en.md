In the past, I often relied on my limited human understanding and feelings to judge the value of everyday matters, growth, and work. Life is so short, unpredictable, and fleeting, and man's abilities, in the face of death, bring about a sense of despair. When confronted with eternity, I felt infinitely small. Thus, I was extremely prone to sinking into a mood where everything seemed empty, meaningless, and worthless.

Looking back, I realize how much time I wasted in such wandering and sighing.

When I turned my focus to God and contemplated "glorifying God and taking joy in Him," light began to shine through. Things and matters I originally deemed meaningless and worthless could now be re-evaluated through the perspective of "whether they glorify God," rather than my own understanding and feelings.

Another point worth being vigilant about is, when deciding to do something, one needs to pause and consider whether it's to glorify oneself or to glorify God. Often, it's about satisfying one's own pride.
