In movies, especially some foreign films, trains always give me a sense of warmth, freedom, relaxation, and hope.
K137, the train back to Changsha, presented a scene that left me "shocked and utterly disgusted."
Many people smoked without any scruples inside the carriage, carelessly threw their trash right onto the floor—melon seed shells, chicken bones, food wrappers.
The whole carriage was filled with smoke, smelled terrible, like a complete junkyard.
I loathed these people, even despised them. "A group of low-class people," I thought to myself.
Silently, I vowed never to take the train back to Changsha again. I simply couldn't bear it. I'd rather not go home.
I know some of my thoughts are not right, but why are they like this?
