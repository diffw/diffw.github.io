"You shouldn't come back; it's a waste of money and work gets delayed. Come back next time." My grandfather had a stroke, teetering on the edge of life, and I had planned to visit, but my father advised me over the phone.
I understood what he meant by "next time" and accepted his advice. This happened last Friday.
All week, I've felt uneasy, frequently sensing a debt.
During breakfast on Tuesday, while doing devotions with my teacher, I mentioned my feelings.
"Of course, it's better to see him while he's alive and give him comfort; what use is it to face a corpse after he's gone?" my teacher reminded me.
I immediately felt clarity in my heart and quickly decided to take the train that night back home. (In fact, the most important thing is to ensure my grandfather affirms the faith he accepted before, accepting Jesus, so that his soul can be saved and go to heaven.)
My heart felt much relieved. Moreover, I realized I tend to get confused by others' opinions. I need to think more independently and act more resolutely.
