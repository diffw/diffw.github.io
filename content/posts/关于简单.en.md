I often fear and reject the idea of "simplicity," worrying that I might be seen as lacking depth or substance, as "not enough." Reflecting on my past lifestyle, my approach to things, my reading habits, and my design work, I realize I've been buried under the weight of others' opinions, going with the flow. In certain late-night moments, or when I'm alone by West Lake, I know deep down that this is not what I truly want. Why? Because living in this monstrous world, I constantly worry about how the world sees me, whether it will accept me. But since, like all living beings, I too will someday leave this world, and since the world's standards and values are transient, why live by these ever-changing standards in this finite and precious life?

I can choose simplicity. I can live more freely and liberated.

I don't need your understanding.
