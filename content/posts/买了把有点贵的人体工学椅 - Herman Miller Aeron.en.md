Since I design and write from home for long periods, I needed a good ergonomic chair. **I hope this chair will last at least 10 years.**

The chair I'm currently using is an IKEA office chair I bought for my daughter, which cost 300 yuan. It’s definitely a usable chair, but not comfortable. I've tried some chairs at work that cost three to four thousand yuan, and they were okay—not particularly uncomfortable or comfortable.

Because I'm aiming for a 10-year use, I wanted to experience what a top-tier ergonomic chair feels like. So, I made an appointment at the Herman Miller showroom in Shanghai (Address: Building 2, 550 Jumen Road, Luwan District, Shanghai).

The showroom displayed almost all of Herman Miller’s products. The staff was friendly and professional, guiding me through their star products: Embody, Aeron, Mirra 2, and Sayl. **Though Embody is the highest-end, I personally found Aeron and Mirra 2 more comfortable.** Sayl was not acceptable; it felt too rigid. So, if I were to choose this brand, it would be between Aeron and Mirra 2.

That day happened to be June 18th. The staff mentioned that there was a sale on JD.com with significant discounts (2000-3500 yuan), advising to buy now or wait until Singles' Day. I was skeptical, thinking it was just a sales gimmick to raise prices before discounting them.

Interestingly, the Herman Miller showroom only offers experiences, not sales. Purchases must be made through their website or on Tmall/JD.com. Additionally, Herman Miller chairs come with a 12-year warranty.

Initially, I planned to visit Okamura’s showroom as well. Some of Okamura’s products are much cheaper than Herman Miller. However, I pondered: for a product intended for 10 years of use, what’s the difference between 5000 and 10000 yuan? Over 10 years, the average cost difference is small, but the experience and body support can be vastly different. **I need to train myself to consider pricing and value from a long-term perspective.** This is not easy.

If financially feasible, why not choose top-tier products? After all, it’s important to treat oneself well 😄. So, I decided on the Aeron.

Given the 618 sale, I thought I’d try my luck. For the first time, I participated in JD.com’s 618 shopping festival (I haven't really tried Tmall’s Singles' Day). Originally priced at 12,010 yuan (with 365 yuan shipping), the JD.com 618 sale offered it for 8,855 yuan, saving 3,520 yuan. After 618, I noticed that the original price indeed returned to 12,010 yuan. If friends plan to buy an HM chair, wait for Singles' Day; they genuinely offer real deals, not gimmicks.

After the chair arrived, I took some time to adjust its parameters according to my height, posture, and desk height. It truly became my personal chair (others might not find it as comfortable as I do). After setting it up, it occurred to me that if Herman Miller could save multiple user configurations for their chairs with a quick switch option, it would be fantastic.

Lastly, I borrow a picture (and a Zhihu answer) from **Xindong Network CEO Huang Yimeng** to show you **how to quickly judge the quality of an ergonomic chair:** Check the armrests. On a good chair, the armrests are attached to the backrest and will tilt with it; on a lower-quality chair, the armrests are attached to the seat.
