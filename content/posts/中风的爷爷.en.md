I have always been afraid of the dead or the dying. But when I saw my grandpa, who wasn't fully conscious, I didn't feel afraid. He was still alive, though his right eye was already cloudy.

With my mouth close to his ear, I told him loudly that I had come to see him. My aunt said it was no use; he couldn't distinguish anymore.

When I held his hand, cradled his head, and pressed my forehead against his face, I noticed a tear rolling down from the corner of his left eye.

Not only was he alive, but he was also very aware inside.

Our family says they don't think grandpa will hold on much longer.

But I can't shake the feeling that he will get better.

I think about how wonderful it would be to see grandpa laughing heartily again.

I prayed for him, hoping he would accept Jesus. I'm really lacking faith in this; not only do I have no faith, but I also feel immense pressure. My wife tells me that God will be at work in this.

I think she's right.
