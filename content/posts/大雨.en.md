Swiftly and irresistibly descending from over ten thousand meters above, the rain pierced through clouds and air, relentlessly pelting everything on the ground.

This was yesterday's heavy rain.

I stopped what I was doing, opened the window, and watched the rain. It had been a long time since I had calmly and attentively watched the rain. Never before had I noticed how raindrops merge into streams, joyfully flowing down the eaves. A gust of wind came, carrying the refreshing scent of a sun-scorched earth washed clean by the rain, reminding me of a past rain, vague yet profound.

I wanted to tell someone about this rain, only to realize there was no one to share it with.

I laughed to myself.
