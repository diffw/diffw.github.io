On my way to the restroom, I suddenly realized that in my current and future life, I will never have the neatly organized blocks of time I had in my youth. Instead, what I have now are countless fragments of time—1 hour, 30 minutes, 10 minutes, 5 minutes, 2 minutes...

Recognizing this, I see that my long-held belief of "waiting for a fixed block of time to thoroughly do something" is extremely flawed.

No wonder I've always complained about having so many ideas but never enough time.

In reality, the time has always been there. It's just not like in college, where I had entire months or weeks, or those early workdays, with whole days and nights free.

Now, my time is in fragments: the hour when the baby is sound asleep, or a moment when my wife doesn't need help with chores, or the 15-minute lunch break at work...

Having realized this, how do I use these scattered pieces of time? How do I plant my plans and dreams within these fragments?

Branded store, illustration creation, graphic design, English, theoretical studies, ...

Waiting to continue thinking...
