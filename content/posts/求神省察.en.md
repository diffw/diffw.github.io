Jan 9, 2021 at 7:57 AM

May your whole spirit, soul, and body be preserved blameless at the coming of our Lord Jesus Christ. (1 Thessalonians 5:23)

Waking up early, I realized a problem. Yesterday was Friday, and I seemed to have let loose; I was supposed to go to bed at 10:30 PM, but instead, I watched a martial arts movie until midnight, which has always been my entertainment preference. Thus, when the alarm went off at 5:30 AM this morning, I half-consciously rationalized to myself: it's Saturday, let's sleep in. And so, I woke up at 7:30.

Much like the Israelites leaving Egypt, moving on to a new life, yet constantly reminiscing about the days of slavery in Egypt, going back to the "pleasures of sin."

It seems that life in Christ feels like a burden and labor, so there's a need to temporarily set aside these burdens on weekends and holidays and enjoy happiness by worldly standards, sometimes to the point of unrestrained pleasure.

This is the first time I've considered "life in Christ is burdensome." I pray that God helps me to rest, even resting in Christ.

Paul prays for us: May the God of peace Himself sanctify you completely. May your whole spirit, soul, and body be preserved blameless at the coming of our Lord Jesus Christ. (1 Thessalonians 5:23)

No one can preserve their spirit, soul, and body in holiness to be completely blameless before the perfectly holy and righteous God. To make me (us) completely sanctified (reaching God's standard of holiness) is God's personal work. So, what should I do? I think it should be loving God with all my heart, soul, and mind. We must exert all our efforts, using our thoughts, emotions, intellect, and will to love God. Just relying on emotion and feeling is not enough.

Following Brother Chambers' recommendation, I read Psalm 139 and was moved by God's preservation of me (us), beyond our imagination:

1 O Lord, you have searched me and known me.

2 You know when I sit down and when I rise up;

you discern my thoughts from afar;

...

13 For you formed my inward parts;

you knitted me together in my mother's womb.

...

16 Your eyes saw my unformed body.

All the days ordained for me were written in your book

before one of them came to be.

Gratitude:

1. Had a conversation with Brother L in the morning, and have basically made a decision;

2. Stuck to the 5:30 AM devotional and 5 chapters of the Bible every day for a week. Thank God for His guidance; (indeed, I also put in effort, using willpower and strength to persist. But the willingness and being moved during devotion is the Holy Spirit's work within me)

3. Communicated with Brother YT. (Read 1 Thessalonians 5:12-13 Brothers, we urge you to recognize those who labor among you, and are over you in the Lord and admonish you, and to esteem them very highly in love for their work's sake. Be at peace among yourselves.)
