Jan 9, 2021 at 7:57 AM

May your whole spirit, soul, and body be kept blameless at the coming of our Lord Jesus Christ. (1 Thessalonians 5:23, NIV)

Woke up this morning and realized something. Yesterday was Friday, and I seemed to slack off. I intended to go to bed by 10:30 PM but ended up watching a martial arts movie until midnight, which has always been my kind of entertainment. This morning, when the alarm went off at 5:30, I sleepily assured myself, "Today is Saturday, so let’s get up a little later." So, when I finally woke up, it was already 7:30.

Like the Israelites who left Egypt, I’ve escaped the past and started a new life, but I still often think about "the days of slavery back in Egypt," wanting to return to "the pleasures of sin."

It seems that living in Christ is a burden, so we feel the need to temporarily set aside these burdens on weekends or holidays and indulge in pleasure by worldly standards, sometimes with no restraint.

This is the first time I’ve thought about the idea that "living in Christ is a burden." I prayed, asking God to help me see that rest can also be found in Christ.

Paul prays for us: May the God of peace Himself sanctify you completely. May your whole spirit, soul, and body be kept blameless at the coming of our Lord Jesus Christ. (1 Thessalonians 5:23, NIV)

No one can keep their spirit, soul, and body pure enough to be blameless in front of the completely holy and just God. To make me (us) fully sanctified (to reach God's holiness standard) is God's work alone. So what should I do? I think it should be to love God with all my heart, soul, mind, and strength. I should use my thoughts, emotions, intelligence, and willpower to love God wholeheartedly. Just relying on feelings and emotions isn't enough.

Following Oswald Chambers' suggestion, I read Psalm 139 and was touched by God's care for me (for us), far beyond what we can imagine:

   the days fashioned for me,
   when as yet there were none of them.
