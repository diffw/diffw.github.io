### The Power of a Gospel Tract

In 1906, at the age of 20, James Outrum Fraser, a Christian born into a wealthy family in England and skilled in mathematics and music, was deeply moved by a gospel tract given to him by a classmate. A short message on that tract captured his heart and changed his life's course:

> If our Lord were to return today and find millions upon millions of people still unreached by the gospel, still seeking, He would surely question us and demand an answer. I truly do not know how we would respond. But one thing I am sure of is that the many excuses we now make with a seared conscience will utterly shame us!

After graduating from university, Fraser promptly applied to the China Inland Mission, founded by Hudson Taylor, for missionary work. One aspect that attracted him to the Mission was their policy of not soliciting funds or relying on anyone but God alone for finances.

### The Cost of Faith

With great knowledge, Fraser moved from the civilized city of London to the primitive and uncultivated regions of the Lisu people in Yunnan, China. This required him to abandon all that he had learned in England. In the Lisu area, he faced poor living conditions (primitive, dirty, and messy), external dangers (being hunted by tribesmen), dietary customs vastly different from the West (unacceptable even to many Han Chinese), dwelling conditions (sleeping on the ground), language barriers, and a profound sense of physical and spiritual loneliness (he was alone in his missionary work for a long time). Furthermore, the Lisu people were inconsistent in their faith (quick to believe but equally quick to backslide repeatedly), and he saw little success in his work for five years in China. His friends, family, and even other missionaries often misunderstood him (his estranged elder brother mocked him on a rare visit).

In contrast, my faith life and that of my generation (whether city or rural, especially city dwellers) is much too easy. We need to have a heart willing to pay the cost.

### Sharing the Gospel Anytime, Anywhere

Fraser was always ready to share the gospel on the streets, seizing every opportunity. To spread the gospel, he eagerly engaged in conversations with passersby and would walk for days into remote villages to share God's message. Out of love, he and his coworkers helped a severely beaten little girl, and they often carried injured strangers to receive care, appearing "meddlesome" to some. These acts revealed to the Lisu people that he was different.

He came for the faith of the Lisu people in Jesus, a burden from the Great Commission pressing upon him. As he wrote in his diary, he hoped that this burden would also weigh on every Christian. Truly, I owe a debt to the gospel.

### The Power of Prayer

Despite a busy missionary life, Fraser dedicated the most valuable part of his day to prayer. He wrote: In the past, I thought that prayer was first and teaching was second. Now I realize that first is prayer, second is prayer, third is prayer, and only fourth is teaching.

While convalescing in Shanghai, Fraser developed a precious friendship with Dixon Hoste, one of the "Cambridge Seven" and the then General Director of the China Inland Mission. Hoste told Fraser that "praying more can help save time and energy; not praying results in wasting time and energy." Greatly influenced by this elder, Fraser joined him in daily prayers, often lasting for hours.

One of Fraser's prayer methods involved writing out prayer topics like sermon outlines and then praying over them. This truly is a good method.

### The Power of Intercession

At that time, the Lisu region was rife with demon worship and idolatry, and Fraser, alone, needed co-workers. Although the China Inland Mission provided ample support, they couldn’t arrange for co-workers. Fraser wrote to his mother in London, requesting that Christians in London form a dedicated, long-term prayer group.

Fraser regularly updated this prayer group on his work and status, writing objectively about victories and failures. Thus, despite being far away in London, the prayer group felt closely connected to Fraser’s ministry work among the Lisu. Fraser repeatedly expressed that his work there was inseparable from the prayer group.

The book mentions a detail that touched me greatly. When Fraser sent letters to London, it would take 6 weeks to travel from the mountains of Yunnan to London. Thus, many times, the events prayed for by the prayer group had already happened and become history. Even so, Fraser continued to insist that the team persist in prayer and believed in the effects of those prayers.

### The Power of God

In Fraser's first five years in Yunnan, there was little success in his ministry. During this time, he was in despair and even had suicidal thoughts, but ultimately resisted the devil through reliance on the words of the Bible.

Afterward, he focused on working hard, planting seeds, and nurturing growth, all while waiting patiently for the harvest without knowing when it would come.

Finally, a wave of the gospel came. Originally, Fraser only planned to visit "Tanch'a," where he had previously served, briefly. If he found no change in the faith situation, he was willing to go work elsewhere for another year or two. But as he "accidentally" visited, it sparked a gospel revival: families and entire villages came to faith, abandoning and burning their idols.

The described revival in the book was truly heartening and inspiring for future generations. Returning to reality, current times, and personal life, one may often not see such a powerful display of God. Why? Sometimes it even leads to discouragement, disappointment, and doubt.

The testimony of Brother Fraser gives me great encouragement: do not lose heart, keep faith in God; work, sow, nurture like a farmer, and patiently await the harvest.

### Don't Underestimate the Power of Satan

Among the Lisu, every household had idol altars; Fraser's ministry there was a direct confrontation with the devil. He was essentially snatching souls from the hands of Satan. Often, the faith journey for the Lisu was tumultuous: they would believe in Jesus, abandon idols, and then leave Jesus to return to their former lives. Throughout this process, Fraser frequently encountered experiences of exorcising demons in the name of Jesus.

As Christians, our lives can seem quite ordinary (I imagine many Christians feel the same). Life presents challenges: work difficulties, interpersonal relationships, family relationships, health issues, etc. These can all be brought into prayer, and it seems that God addresses them.

However, faith and prayer often seem to simply address real-world problems rather than engage as the Bible's Ephesians 6:12 describes, "against the spiritual forces of evil in the heavenly realms."

> For our struggle is not against flesh and blood, but against the rulers, against the authorities, against the powers of this dark world and against the spiritual forces of evil in the heavenly realms.

I think it's not that there isn't "spiritual warfare," but rather we've been deceived. When I fail to evangelize to my parents, I claim there is no satanic power, yet my mother secretly practices idolatry. Among other issues, when I often feel empty and depressed, perhaps it is also Satan's deceit.

Ephesians 6:11: Put on the full armor of God so that you can take your stand against the devil's schemes.

Amen.

### Appendix: Introduction to James O. Fraser (Encyclopedia)

James Outram Fraser (1886 - September 25, 1938), also translated as Fu Leise. British missionary of the China Inland Mission, known as the "Apostle to the Lisu"; creator of the old Lisu script; lived, evangelized, and served among the Lisu people in the border regions of Yunnan, China, for nearly 30 years, ultimately giving his life for the Lisu people.
