Feb 20, 2021 at 6:27 AM

…Arise, let us go hence. (John 14:31 KJV)

The Lord Jesus sometimes had quiet moments "going up into the mountain to pray," and at other times took decisive actions, like "overturning the tables of the money changers," or going "up to Jerusalem, to suffer many things from the elders, chief priests, and scribes, be killed, and rise again on the third day." Whether it was quiet meditation and prayer or resolute action, Jesus was always following the will of the Father. "As the Father commanded me, so I do."

It's right and proper to pray quietly before God and seek His will before putting an idea into action.

However, if God clearly reveals His will to me through the words of the Bible, but I still hesitate, am indecisive, or repeatedly ask or pray again, it indicates that I do not trust God. At that point, more prayer becomes a disguise and an escape.

I have experienced many occasions of "understanding God's will but still avoiding action and merely daydreaming."

1 Corinthians 9:16, "For if I preach the gospel, I have nothing to boast of, for necessity is laid upon me; yes, woe is me if I do not preach the gospel!" I felt compelled to share the gospel with a friend, but hesitated because the excuse was that he wasn't ready or the timing was not right...

Ecclesiastes 7:7, "A bribe blinds those who see and twists the words of the righteous." When learning to drive, I understood what God's will was but still wrestled for a long time over whether to bribe the instructor. (Ultimately, I did not bribe.)

Malachi 3:10, "Bring all the tithes into the storehouse, that there may be food in My house, and try Me now in this,’ says the Lord of hosts, ‘if I will not open for you the windows of heaven and pour out for you such blessing that there will not be room enough to receive it.’" When I received a large sum apart from my salary, I hesitated for half a year. I'd already given my tithes, wasn't that enough? We don't need to follow Old Testament rules anymore, right? Can't I just give a little according to my heart rather than a full tithe? (Since a tithe would be a large amount.) Can the church handle this money responsibly, or should I manage it myself...

By faith, Abraham, when called to go to a place he would later receive as his inheritance, obeyed and went, even though he did not know where he was going. (Hebrews 11:8 KJV)

To heed, to act, without hesitation or idle dreaming, is Abraham's belief and trust in God.

In contrast, consider Lot's behavior.

With the coming of dawn, the angels urged Lot, saying, "Hurry! Take your wife and your two daughters who are here, or you will be swept away when the city is punished." Yet Lot delayed. (Genesis 19:15-16 KJV)

God's urgency, and His will were so clear and yet Lot "delayed," which shows his unbelief.

"Not lingering in idle dreams" is also a process of growth in faith. Even though there have been past failures, may the Lord renew me today, giving me the faith to listen and act according to His word.

Gratitude

1. Thank God for guiding me in yesterday's product design discussion with the WD team, resulting in a clear plan. I enjoyed the process of team members fully expressing and contributing their perspectives, culminating in a shared conclusion. Whether in product design or UX, I must acknowledge that my vision and thinking are inevitably limited and incomplete. Sometimes, they may even be overly complex or overdesigned. Therefore, at these times, I need the team’s help—different minds offering feedback, reminders, and additions to approach the same problem from various angles. I am grateful to God because I prayed over the discussion's process and outcome beforehand, asking for a humble and open heart. The discussion lasted nearly two hours, reaching a stalemate until a breakthrough idea emerged from one of the members, which the whole team seized upon. A clear path suddenly materialized before us. I must say, this inspiration was from God.
2. Thank God for leading over a dozen families in our church to study "The Hearts of Children, The Heart of Parents." When disciplining children, we tend to focus on correcting behaviors, often neglecting the heart behind the actions and missing the chance to help the children from their hearts.
3. Thank God for guiding our family worship at 7:00 PM. Together, we read the scriptures I studied in the morning. I then shared the morning's spiritual insights, connecting them with events in our family that the children could understand, with my wife and children.
