It's a family tradition for us to recount and share the past year's blessings on Thanksgiving. One evening during the week of Thanksgiving, our whole family gathers to project onto the TV all the photos and videos from my iPhone over the past year. We each write down events or moments we are thankful for whenever they come to mind. After two hours, each person usually has accumulated between twenty to thirty items of gratitude. Life moves fast, our minds often overflow with worldly matters, and it's so easy to forget the grace we have received from God. We need this intentional review and counting. Over the next few days, we organize our lists; finally, on Thanksgiving evening, after praise and prayer, we take turns standing up and reading our lists aloud.

I believe that counting God's grace in this way is very worthwhile.

On another day, I attended a Thanksgiving gathering with close friends. Although they didn't precisely document God's grace in everyday life as we do, their impromptu reflections moved me. They reflected on how God had changed them over the past year or the hardships they faced and how God provided grace during those times. I realized that while daily thankfulness is one aspect, I also need to deeply contemplate and reflect on how God has changed me personally, focusing more on the internal rather than just the external. Moreover, when writing about my thankfulness, I need to introspect and be cautious of the motives behind publishing it. It's incredibly easy for it to subtly shift from counting God's blessings to boasting. May the Lord help.

1. Grateful for the peace, joy, and hope God has given me. Over the past year, I've had minor health issues, like persistent tinnitus in my right ear and worsening vision (early signs of presbyopia), along with a significant decline in physical and mental energy—essentially, "my body is gradually decaying." Beyond that, at forty, my inner self struggles with various conflicts. Questions about life's direction and value and doubts about life's meaning bring feelings of emptiness and confusion (despite being a Christian). Being abroad also occasionally adds to the identity dilemma—although this isn't a constant issue, it's still a concern. I wonder how I would face these challenges without God and His words and promises. Therefore, I'm thankful because when I bring these issues into prayer and meditation, peace, joy, and hope flow from within. Even when I haven't prayed about these specific matters, as long as I turn to God and read His word, I feel at peace.

2. Thankful for God's love, faithfulness, and unchanging love. To some extent, I fully realize how great and filthy my sins are. I realize this because, despite my weakness, when I'm indulging in the pleasures of sin, I still feel the pain of being bound by sin and the conflict within me between the flesh and the Spirit. Thanks be to God's love—He actually loves a rebellious, repeatedly sinning sinner like me. I am a reborn and saved person through the blood of Jesus Christ, and each act of sin hurts God the Father again. But this Father, with unchanging love, continually shows mercy and grace, forgives me, and grants me liberation and freedom from sin. Often, when I'm angry because my children repeatedly fail to change or improve, the Spirit within reminds me of God's loving treatment towards a rebellious child like me, which humbles and shames me.

3. Grateful for God's preservation and guidance in my relationship with Him. I'm far from what I deem the standard of a devout Christian. I'm not setting a "pious standard" as a law. I just feel indebted to God because I don't pursue Him, love Him, long for His word, or love others. Thanks be to God for upholding our relationship. I often resemble the prodigal son who left the Father, lost in the world, but God often brings me back to His embrace. Sometimes it's the Spirit's reminders within, or the inspiration from reading the Bible or hearing it occasionally, or reminders through people or circumstances.

4. Grateful for God's protection over my marriage. In 2023, several friends around me, both Christians and non-Christians, faced various marriage problems and challenges: cold wars, distant relationships, warning signals, divorces, etc. Truthfully speaking, my marriage can be considered normal and stable at this point, but potential problems and crises still exist. Christians believe marriage is ordained by God and has many meanings for individuals. One important meaning is to let oneself be fully exposed in the sight of their spouse, allowing them to become a mirror reflecting the sins, ugliness, and selfishness within, as well as pain/loss experienced during growth. Marriage failure means the failure to address or avoid these issues. A marriage still functioning normally does not mean these issues are gone. Couples need to be vigilant. Genesis 2:24-25 describes a perfect marriage state: two people united, naked but unashamed. Simply put, couples should be united both physically and spiritually, open and without secrets. This is the state I expect for my marriage. Within marriage, everyone hopes to be loved, understood, and accepted, but without God, no one has the capacity to love, understand, and accept. Thank you, God, for protecting my marriage.

5. Grateful for God's preservation of our family relationships. In our small four-person household, four sinners are together, with joys, tears, hurts, and healing in the spouse, parent-child, and sibling relationships. Thank you, God, for preservation.

6. Grateful for God's provision of everything our family needs. The Bible says we should be content with food and clothing. Clearly, we have more than that. Thank you, God, for providing. Though I often worry, am greedy, love the world, and find security in money, may the Lord help me worship God, not wealth.

7. Grateful for God's provision of educational resources (school/sports activities) for the children. Previously in Shanghai, the children's education was chaotic. At one point, it took an hour and a half to drive one way from Pudong to Songjiang, going back and forth four times a day. Now, all educational activities are a 10-minute drive one way. Thank you, God.

8. Grateful for R4C Chinese Church's pastoral care of our family. There are certain differences between Chinese churches in China and the USA, and I'm still adapting. My slow-warming, cautious, conservative personality makes this adaptation process slower. I thank the R4C Church. Over the past year, although I haven't gained much from sermons, which I admit is partly my issue, the pastor and his wife's love, passion for evangelism, and the love from church brothers and sisters have been nurturing in a different way.

9. Grateful for our gatherings at Story Church. Before the 11:45 R4C Chinese Church service, we attend the 9:00 English service at Story Church. I enjoy worshipping God with people of different ethnicities. God is not just the God of the Chinese. But when you stay in a monocultural environment too long, it becomes challenging to equate Jesus and Jesus. I also enjoy Story Church's worship music, simple with guitar and hand drums, often traditional hymns, frequently moving me through the collective worship and lyrics.

10. Grateful to God for providing timely treatment for Linda's acute allergic reaction through caring strangers. Whenever family and friends ask me about Americans, I always mention this incident. One evening, my daughter Linda was semi-conscious from an acute allergy. A stranger mom left her child to gather several other unknown dads to lift my daughter into a car. This mom led my wife to the hospital. Thank you, God, and thank you, these caring strangers.

11. Grateful to God for protecting Aaron's eyes from injury. I had tied a rope around a post while tidying the yard. Without proper consideration, its height was nearly at Aaron's head level. Despite warnings not to run, he forgot. It was truly a close call that almost resulted in irreparable injury to his eye. The thought is terrifying. Thank you, God, for protection. When his scar healed, I specifically took a photo to commemorate God’s care, lest we, as ungrateful people, forget or deny God's grace later.

12. Grateful to God for providing immediate treatment for Linda's accidental foot injury. I'd shared God's provision for our family's needs, enjoying many benefits as Christians. Yet we face danger and crisis too. My daughter stepped on a sewing needle, which broke into pieces inside her foot. Grateful for God's protection, His guidance to quickly discern her symptoms without imaging and rush to the hospital instead of following friends' well-meaning advice to wait at home. Also thankful God answered our prayers, preparing medical resources by moving a week-later surgery to the next day. In between hospitals, I was indeed fatigued and anxious, but grateful for the peace God provided.

13. Grateful to God for guiding me to a Christian event in New York in May. Different ethnic Christians shared their ministry stories (serving campuses, prisons, shelters, etc.), providing inspiration and encouragement. A Black Christian elder serving the homeless said that compared to traditional service, working in Internet technology is akin to having Iron Man's superpowers—utilize them well.

14. Grateful to God for guiding me on a hiking trip in Colorado in June. Seven or eight Christian brothers from various cities set aside work and family, heading to a remote, no-phone-signal, electricity-free area in Colorado's wilderness. We hiked during the day, sometimes encountering rain and hail, and gathered around campfires in the evening to share life stories about faith, marriage, and careers. The most beautiful part was half a day alone in a forest-surrounded open meadow, contemplating God's creation and salvation.

15. Grateful to God for Jason & Elizabeth's family's care. This couple from Story Church has warmly helped immigrant Chinese families like ours.

16. Grateful to God for leading us to a retreat in Thailand in July, and our subsequent gathering with friends in the Lord.

17. Grateful to God for blessing us with our third child, ensuring smooth progress during pregnancy, and safe birth of daughter Esther. It seems every time we move to a new city, a new child is born. In 2010, 2014, and 2023, our three children were born in Hangzhou, Shanghai, and Los Angeles respectively. This time, I was in the delivery room to welcome her birth firsthand. When the doctor connected the child to the umbilical cord and placed her before me, I was in awe, trembling at God's great creation.

18. Grateful to God for the care from Teacher Song & his wife. This elderly couple often drives dozens of kilometers to visit us, worried we are busy, always saying they'll only stay for a few minutes to talk and pray with us.

19. Grateful to God for guiding daughter Linda’s baptism. This is a very significant step in her life, and gratitude and glory to God. Pray that her faith grows, and her heart stays true to God's path.

20. Grateful to God for leading me into Covenant Church's Trinity Gospel activity. Through R4C Church's evangelism training and practice, I've gradually sensed the significance and preciousness of Christian faith to human life. Woe is me if I do not preach the gospel.

21. Grateful to God for ensuring safe travel back to China with my son in late December to meet with friends from the Shanghai church. Over the year, there have been many changes, events, growths, challenges, and stories. Gathering with everyone again, the familiar atmosphere and emotions remain.

22. Grateful to God for preserving us during a brief reunion with Grandma. Arrangements were rushed, and after a few hours together, we left hurriedly. Seeing Grandma's reluctance to part, I felt deeply indebted.

23. Grateful to God for guiding me to attend house church in Changsha. The last gathering here was in 2018. Reconnecting with Elder Zheng from five years ago, there was no estrangement. Seeing Elder Zheng's face full of Christ's glory and joy, as he welcomed elderly attendees on Sunday morning, touched me deeply. Attending two gatherings, I met this church's preacher, and surprisingly, we share mutual friends. Among Christian brothers, even first-time conversations are barrier-free because we are united in the Lord. Very moved by the church's fervency amidst difficulty, strengthening them. Chinese churches in the U.S. and China face different challenges and crises. May the Lord help me fulfill my role in the American church as best as I can.

24. Grateful to God for leading me to preach the Gospel to my parents, brother, and sister-in-law. Previously, my faith couldn’t "cross provinces." In Shanghai, I was a Christian; each return to Changsha rendered me faithless, unable to live out my belief or mention my faith, let alone share the Gospel with family. Thank God, I've changed. During family interactions this time, I strived to seize opportunities to share Gospel messages. The night before my departure, I stayed seriously in my parents' room, telling them about Christ's Gospel with relative completeness, though they were not yet ready to accept it. I trust in God's guidance and preparation. Ultimately, I suggested a blessing prayer for them; slightly embarrassed, they didn't refuse, so I pronounced a blessing prayer for them.
