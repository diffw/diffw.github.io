My aunt sent my cousin to Hangzhou, hoping I could have some influence on him. Faced with a nineteen-year-old who is immersed in fantasy novels, has poor self-control, and constantly causes his parents headaches, I initially had some "educational plans," but soon, I felt helpless. How difficult it is to change a person.

I told my wife, what if we had a son like this? Perhaps I would have given up long ago.

Deep down, I really started to give up, at least, I didn't think I could change him in any way or have any positive influence on him. But I know my aunt hasn't given up on him, despite years of disappointment; I also know God hasn't given up on him, no matter how troublesome my cousin is, he remains His child, just like me. Actually, we should be thankful because, although my cousin is obsessed with novels and lacks direction, he hasn't committed any major wrongs.

Currently, the only thing I'm sure about is if he can be changed, only God can do it. Besides praying, what else can I do? Although during this time, a continuous week of praying did not result in the fulfillment I hoped for, leading to deep complaints against God, even to the point of ignoring His existence.

I know my cousin is a lesson for me.
