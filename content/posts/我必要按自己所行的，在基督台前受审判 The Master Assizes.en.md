For we must all appear before the judgment seat of Christ, so that each of us may receive what is due us for the things done while in the body, whether good or bad. (2 Corinthians 5:10 NIV)

When Christ returns, He will judge me according to my deeds on earth.

What are the outcomes of our deeds, whether good or bad, under the judgment of Christ?

For those who do not accept Christ’s salvation (i.e., non-Christians), the outcome of the judgment is surely condemnation and eternal punishment.

For Christians, will Christ condemn me based on my deeds? No! Because, through Jesus' salvation, I am already seen as a righteous person by God, without guilt. If that’s the case, then what is the result of the judgment for Christians?

1 Corinthians 3:13, 14, 15 makes it very clear.

13 Their work will be shown for what it is, because the Day will bring it to light. It will be revealed with fire, and the fire will test the quality of each person’s work. 14 If what has been built survives, the builder will receive a reward. 15 If it is burned up, the builder will suffer loss but yet will be saved—even though only as one escaping through the flames. (1 Corinthians 3:13-15 NIV)

Christ will evaluate our work, that is, He will judge our actions in life. If our deeds are pleasing to God and can withstand the test of fire, the work will remain, and we will receive Christ’s reward. Conversely, if our deeds are evil, although we can still be saved, it will be like escaping through the flames.

I have never experienced "escaping through flames," but I can imagine the danger and pain it entails. While both result in salvation, one receives Christ’s reward, while the other is an escape through fire, making the wise choice obvious.

But I have been unwise. At least, until this morning, I was unwise.

My inner foolishness, ignorance, and worldly desires made me want to be just a 60-point Christian, just passing. In other words, just obtaining this "green card" to the Kingdom of Heaven is enough. The "extra" reward from Christ seems like an embellishment I don’t really need. Pleasing myself in this world is more important than pleasing God.

Thank God for today’s message, a wake-up call for someone as unwise as I am. Christians with a "60% passing" mindset may be saved, but it is like escaping through fire.

Think about it; what is the work in my hands now? Are my actions, motives, and attitudes pleasing to God? Am I earnestly caring for those I should be concerned about?

The other day, I listened to "Value" by Lei Zhang, a renowned investor in China. He mentioned a profound realization: Anything that can be destroyed by fire is not important, such as houses, money, or other material wealth. Things that cannot be burned are what truly matter, summarized in a person’s knowledge, abilities, and values.

I’m not sure if Lei Zhang is a Christian, but his words reflect a lot of Christian belief.
