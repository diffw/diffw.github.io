Jesus said: Feed my sheep. (John 21:17 NIV)

Peter's love for Jesus was purified through his complete failure of denying the Lord three times before the rooster crowed. Before the Lord, he had nothing to boast about or declare, only a complete denial of himself. He was renewed.

Jesus asked Peter three times, "Do you love me?" Peter answered Him three times, "Lord, you know that I love you." In response, Jesus told Peter three times, "Feed my sheep."

A Christian who has experienced "loving God with human love and utterly failing," when renewed, says to the Lord Jesus, with shame, humility, and trembling, "Lord, you know that I love you." The Lord Jesus's charge, demand, and command are—"Feed my sheep."

Why not other charges or commands? For instance, live well, take care of others, change the world?

Feeding my sheep is truly the most basic, ordinary, exhausting task, utterly unexciting with no future prospects or sense of achievement (a bit like planting trees one by one in the desert), yet it’s also the most important, greatest, and sacred task, because this is the will of the Father.

Jesus was also sent into the world because of His love for the sheep—love for mankind. Jesus said, "but so that the world will know that I love the Father and do exactly what my Father has commanded me." Jesus’s way of loving the Father was to do what the Father commanded. And we, in our love for the Lord Jesus, must also follow His commands.

Is "Feed my sheep" said to all Christians, or only to "high-level" Christians like Peter who shepherd and lead the church? I think it is meant for all Christians.

For Christians at different life stages and in different service contexts, feeding my sheep may take different forms. Jesus's sheep include those already in the fold and those still outside. Preaching the gospel, caring for the young/the weak, helping the mature to become leaders, these are all ways of "feeding my sheep."
