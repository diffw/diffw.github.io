Jan 14, 2021 at 5:58 AM

Who will I send? Who will go for us? I said, Here I am, send me. (Isaiah 6:8)

God's calling is something I have paid close attention to since becoming a Christian, and as I grow older, I care more about it. I've even had a somewhat extreme view, believing that God has designated a unique mission for me (and everyone), a special task. Someday, I would receive a call.

As a result, I've been waiting, but not with hope and joy.

Through Brother Chambers’ sharing, I gained a deeper understanding of this familiar verse.

God's call, "Who will I send? Who will go for us?" was neither directed at a specific person (Isaiah) nor did it "reveal" specific tasks. It was only after Isaiah responded, "Here I am, send me," that God spoke of the specific tasks.

It seems that from God's perspective, His calling is, first and foremost, for someone who is willing to respond with "Here I am, send me," without asking what the task is, someone willing to simply offer themselves. Just as Abraham went out, following God's instructions, without knowing where he was going. (Hebrews 11:8)

However, I have focused on "the tasks of the calling." On deeper reflection, I actually hope to realize my value through a task. Digging even deeper, I may seek to prove my worth by accomplishing a task. In this era, "realizing one's own value" sounds perfectly normal and superficially differs little from "responding to God's call," but in truth, the difference is vast.

The difference lies in whether one offers oneself as a living sacrifice, completely denying oneself, without personal thoughts, ready to be sent.

Brother Chambers finally reminded me not to believe that God will push or plead with you. Indeed, in some services and external needs, I've often had such foolish and arrogant thoughts.

Grateful for:

1. Conversations with Pastor X. Regarding the role of parents in a child's faith, he mentioned that whether a child will become a believer is God's work. Parents should live out their own faith, be good witnesses, and continuously teach their children through the Bible (and its principles), avoiding negative examples that could cause them to stumble. He also shared valuable insights on family time management and personal vision.
2. A deep discussion and exchange with my daughter on a particular matter. When the views between the parents and daughter became confrontational, and emotions were rising, I suggested we each pray and then communicate again. Forty minutes later, my daughter came out with a chart she had completed after the prayer. Her subsequent statements became very calm (so I had to confirm twice: is this really what you think? You don’t need to say things against your will to please us). Thank God for guiding Linda’s growth, making her heart often soft and peaceful.
3. Thank God for preserving our recent family life as we experience a life relying on God.
