Jan 27, 2021 at 6:24 AM

So I tell you, do not worry about what you will eat or drink for life; or about what you will wear for the body. Is not life more than food, and the body more than clothes? (Matthew 6:25 NIV)

Now, what am I worried about?

I worry about my health. Nowadays, severe diseases like cancer and tumors are common. Several friends around me have experienced or are going through terminal illnesses, and I have witnessed the passing of a few friends. A string is always tight in my heart—when will this misfortune come upon me? Likewise, I worry about misfortune befalling my wife, children, parents...

I worry about finances. It's not due to lack, because we have enough—God has provided sufficiently. But I worry about how to earn more.

I worry about my children's education. The path we've chosen is special and risky, subject to interruptions and changes. Are they really learning and growing? In the future, will they have enough ability to be independent?

I worry about my children's faith. In fact, every time I contemplate this worry, it triggers another question: Why do I want my children to know Christ, to help them know Christ? The reasons I see and think of are these: First, the teachings of the Bible (even the requirements of the law, because of a lack of love); second, the customs and practices of the Christian community (all Christian families around do this); third, moral constraints on the children. With faith in Christ, kids might be more obedient and not stray when they grow up. These are obviously superficial and even incorrect.

I think the right understanding of my children's faith fundamentally depends on the parents' personal relationship with God. As a father, have I personally experienced God? Do I value the salvation of Jesus and rejoice because of it? Just passing on "rumors of you" to the children is clearly insufficient and powerless; "my own eyes have seen you" is needed.

I also worry about my children's health. My son's eczema on his hands hasn't healed since he was little. My daughter's eyes also occasionally have allergies.

I worry that my parents don't know God. They are getting older, and time is really pressing. I have shared the gospel with them many times, but it hasn't worked. In my view, the lifelong beliefs they've formed (which seem to disregard death) and their understanding pose a huge barrier to accepting the gospel.

I worry about my current job. I am really complex. On one hand, there's a shameful "indifference and lack of urgency." Because there's no fear of being "fired," even losing my job wouldn't affect my life. On the other hand, I value my work very much and hope my design ideas and work products will be recognized by others. Thus, I have such a complex worry.

I think my heart at work is not pure—it's not with "clean hands and a pure heart." I haven't seen my work as something "for the Lord," and am still elevating myself, displaying my own "glory."

I worry about the COVID-19 pandemic. When will the pandemic end? It seems it will never fully return to normal. What I'm worried about is the lack of change in life; more worrying is the dullness of life under pandemic restrictions. Originally, there were so many beautiful and joyful places to visit and enjoy.

The Apostle Paul advises me to "be anxious for nothing, but in everything, by prayer and petition, with thanksgiving, present your requests to God. And the peace of God, which transcends all understanding, will guard your hearts and your minds in Christ Jesus."

Lord, keep me today "without worry," in everything by prayer, petition, and thanksgiving. Do not worry about tomorrow; each day has enough trouble of its own.

Gratitude:

1. There has been progress in the WD project design. Thank God. Yesterday, I worked with a mind depending on God, submitting my design ideas to God, and felt very relaxed;
2. After a night's rest, my body (neck) has eased. During the day, it's really important to exercise restraint in work and arrange rest time well;
3. Thank God for his protection. My mom was in a good mood when chatting with me;
