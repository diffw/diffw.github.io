Early morning at seven, on the Zhejiang University court, there's fog.
After half an hour of basketball, the vast court is almost mine to enjoy alone. The mist cannot block the sun's rays, and although there's only a small white dot in the hazy sky, it's clear that it is the sun.

Exercise brings a stretch to the body, deep breaths, and such fresh air. In the moment of running for a layup, I suddenly feel a kind of happiness that fills my heart with immense joy. For a brief moment, I am completely satisfied with my current life. This satisfaction is not derived from material possessions or external circumstances, but from a sense of security and protection from God. Although I don't have much materially, I have no need to worry at all.

A couple of days ago, on Friday and Saturday, I attended a praise and worship seminar in Shanghai. I gained a lot; becoming a worshiper is a lifelong journey.
