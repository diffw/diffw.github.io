Life is so short, and the cost of hesitation is too high.
On September 30th, after studying the Book of Genesis at Zhou's place with Joze, we were heading back to the botanical garden. As our bikes were about to turn onto the village road, I said I hadn't had a satisfying bike ride in a long time. Joze suggested, "How about a lap around West Lake now?" I said, "Sure."

By nightfall, the roads were less crowded, and we sped along, feeling exhilarated, especially when crossing the Su Causeway. We yelled with joy, feeling completely liberated.

In about 45 minutes, we circled the entire West Lake. Along the way, we talked about life, faith, and the future. It was truly delightful.

Once back at the botanical garden, I suggested grabbing a beer. Since coming to appreciate alcohol, I've found that a little drink can elevate moments of exhilaration to their peak. So, beer and peanuts it was. Life often seems this way—the simplest, most honest pairings bring the greatest joy. The refreshing taste of beer combined with the crunch of peanuts was pure bliss.

Before heading home, I said my biggest fear is living the same routine every day, waking up in the morning knowing exactly what's going to happen, completely devoid of any surprises or twists, and thus becoming numb to life. Life needs breakthroughs, a little something different every day. Joze said we should do whatever new idea comes to mind right away, except bad ones.

Indeed, that's how it should be. Thank God.
