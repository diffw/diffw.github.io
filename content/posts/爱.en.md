Every morning, 20 minutes before heading to work, I like to have some quiet time on the tenth-floor balcony, singing hymns. It's peaceful there, with fresh air and no cigarette smoke. After singing two hymns, the cleaning lady came to mop the floor. As she bent over and pushed the mop back and forth, when she came near me, I felt a twinge of superiority, as if thinking, "We're not in the same class." Realizing this, I felt ashamed.

I wondered what it would be like if we swapped places.

I would undoubtedly feel very inferior, just as I do now when confronting people with power and money.

When facing those poorer than I am, those with a "lower status," I don't respect them. Like when I shout for a waiter at a restaurant or never look the cleaning lady in the eye. In those moments, I see them as slaves because subconsciously, I think, "I've paid, so you have to serve me."

Conversely, when facing those richer and with higher status, I know well that in their eyes, I'm nothing, which makes me deeply insecure. In the presence of power, I live like a slave.

How sad.

What right do I have to treat them as slaves?

What right do I have to belittle myself?

In front of God, we are all equal.

The more I thought about it, the more ashamed I felt, even though I was still singing hymns.

It truly disgraced God's love.

I almost wrote down, "They are so pitiful; I should show some concern..." Actually, they aren't pitiful; they earn an honest living with their clean labor. It's just that life isn't easy for them.

A few times on the balcony, an auntie suddenly started chatting with me. At that moment, I felt very awkward, wondering what others would think of me talking to a "cleaning lady."

Truly shameful.

Perhaps among friends, I pass as a decent person, and sometimes I even feel good about myself. But time and again, these experiences remind me of how shallow and vile I am. Realizing this, I don't become insecure because in the complete view of God, what am I?

I need God's love; everyone does.
