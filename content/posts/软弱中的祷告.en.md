Yesterday was Sunday. Although I returned from worship, I still felt very weak.

Anran was sick and couldn't sleep well, constantly crying and fussing. My wife was exhausted from almost staying up all night taking care of the child. For several days before, Anran had been in this state as well, yet my heart was so hardened that I "ignored it and turned a deaf ear." At that moment, I felt moved to pray for him.

So, I began to pray. Anran's crying gradually, gradually softened, and finally, Anran fell asleep. Meanwhile, I too slowly entered into a state of prayer, deeply enjoying speaking with God. In the end, my heart was surprisingly grateful, thankful to God for using the family's illness to bring about a revival of my faith.

> For the sake of Christ, I am content with weaknesses, insults, hardships, persecutions, and calamities. For when I am weak, then I am strong. 
> 2 Corinthians 12:10
