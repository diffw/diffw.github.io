First, let's start with an old anecdote:

A large company implemented a new soap packaging production line but faced a bug: boxes were often left without soap. The company, therefore, hired a postdoctoral researcher at a high cost, spending over a million to successfully solve the issue. When an empty soap box appeared, a mechanical arm automatically detected and removed it. Meanwhile, a small rural business bought the same production line and faced the same problem. The owner solved it by spending 90 yuan on a high-power fan that blew the empty boxes away.

Currently, in my project, I'm encountering the same problem. Following the "traditional design approach" seems "reasonable and watertight," but to the system and the user, it appears complex. What I need is: simple, direct, and effective.

I'm trying to take a different path.

The design method is important; a unique design perspective is even more valuable.
