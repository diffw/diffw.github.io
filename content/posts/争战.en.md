Today, a colleague shared a "mindfulness" meditation during our weekly meeting and led a 10-minute practice session. Although it was declared non-religious, I felt it carried a strong religious form, seemingly close to Buddhism.

Out of respect, I went along with my colleague's arrangement, but I sat quietly and prayed in my heart. During the prayer, I felt a deep sadness. This colleague had actually been introduced to Christianity before, and he had good Christian friends who worked hard to influence him. From the first conversation we had, I wanted to take him to church, but I kept forgetting and never felt ready. As a result, he sought "comfort and fulfillment" from other sources.

This is a spiritual battle. I pray the Lord uses me to fight for you.

> For our struggle is not against flesh and blood, but against the rulers, against the authorities, against the powers of this dark world and against the spiritual forces of evil in the heavenly realms.
> Ephesians 6:12
