Why can't reading the Bible be as regular as having meals every day? I truly can't live on food alone. Food satisfies the needs of the body, but the Bible is food for the soul.

Why do I spend an hour and a half in a one-on-one Bible study every week, besides Sunday service?

Why do we have to prepare so much for Christmas every year? If I really understand Jesus, deeply feel Jesus' love, and truly love Jesus, then spending time on Christmas should be sweet. So why is it a burden for me now?

Why do I always feel the need to prove myself? Maybe it's rooted in deep-seated insecurity. And can't God provide me with these things?

--

Ultimately, my values have not changed; I still rely on myself (relying on myself is moving away from God).

I can't imagine someone backstroking without moving their hands and feet, floating on the water's surface. I don't believe the buoyancy of water can support my heavy body; I don't have faith.

It is the same with God. How should I entrust everything to Him?

I feel a lack of freedom in my heart.

--

Furthermore, what is the purpose of work? What is the purpose of entrepreneurship? What is the purpose of pursuing more money?
