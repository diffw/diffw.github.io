15 But when God, who set me apart from my mother’s womb and called me by His grace, was pleased 16 to reveal His Son in me so that I might preach Him among the Gentiles, my immediate response was not to consult any human being, (Galatians 1:15-16 NIV)

Brother Chambers said that focusing on Jesus and hoping for His coming, rather than expecting Him to arrive in my way, is what it means to "leave room for God."

How much do I hope for Jesus to come? Obviously, not much. In the stable and smooth areas of life, I appear to be "loving the world less," but in reality, it’s a sort of "passive contentment" that is not rooted in Christ, acting within my own capabilities, with a lack of ambition. For the unknown, the problems, and the adversities in life, I fall into a state of numbness and resignation. For instance, my elderly parents are still not believers, yet my prayers and efforts for them are scarce. Another example is my son Anran's persistent eczema, and again, I pray very little about it. It's as if I'm saying, "Let it be, what can I do?" Fundamentally, it's still a lack of faith or little faith in God.

Thank God for setting me apart from my mother's womb and taking the initiative to lead me back to His family at the age of 23. This was a turning point in my life and destiny. I pray that the Lord will help me not to be a "lover of the world." Those who love the world, after experiencing God's grace, want to use God to achieve their own life plans and fulfil their life's purpose. But followers of God, after experiencing His grace, want to "leave everything" to follow Jesus; (Moved by yesterday’s sermon)

Gratitude:

When I lose my temper with the kids, she remains calm, gentle, and comforts me. It works the other way around too. I believe this is God’s guidance and help, and I am thankful for a marriage and family in Christ.
2. I am grateful for hearing the sermon "Leave Everything to Follow Jesus." YT's words are gentle, calm, and sometimes even slightly monotonous, yet also direct (Eileen’s comment) and powerful. Thank God for His Word. Thanks also to the preacher for their hard work;
