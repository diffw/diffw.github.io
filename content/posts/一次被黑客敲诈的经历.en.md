On February 20, 2019, I received an email that was "sent to myself." That is, the sender and the recipient were both from my work email at Liulishuo: diff@liulishuo.com (so, no visible sender information). After a quick glance, my initial judgment was: **someone is extorting me**.

The gist of the email was:

- Your email has been hacked. Changing your password is pointless; the account has been completely controlled.
- You've been **browsing pornography online**, and it's been recorded. On one hand, they recorded what you viewed; on the other, they recorded who you are and your emotions and actions while watching, through the front camera.
- Pay **$1,000 in Bitcoin to "keep quiet"**.
- If you don't pay within 48 hours, the recording of your browsing will be sent to everyone in your email contacts — coworkers, family, friends.

After receiving this email, how did I feel? 

First, I can't deny that about two years ago, I did watch pornography. In the past two years, I haven't watched any, but I've seen scattered images of beautiful women, intentionally or unintentionally. 

So, I was really scared. **If the "hacker" sends videos of my porn browsing to my colleagues, how will they view me?** Perhaps, my friends and colleagues who don't have faith might not take it too seriously; they might just laugh it off. What scares me is how they would view this devoted Christian, that I could be so disgraceful? How would I face them again? (I think of various Christian examples at home and abroad falling due to pornography and immorality.)  
What should I do?

The immediate reaction was to pay the ransom, to buy some silence. $1,000 isn’t a huge number; compared to "reputation," it’s nothing. But quickly, I realized that this kind of extortion is cyclical. If you pay the first ransom, you can expect to pay a second. But maybe they really just want a one-time payment? This dilemma...

While feeling anxious, the story of David’s adultery and sin from the Bible popped into my mind. Then, some questions arose: **Are you a Christian? Do you believe God is omnipotent, omnipresent, and all-knowing? Are you afraid of your disgraceful actions being known to others, but not afraid of them being known to God? God already knows. You might cover up your faults before others, but can you cover them up before God?**

Faced with these questions, my previous fear of my scandal being revealed slowly faded. I felt ashamed and saddened. I truly am a person who doesn't know God; I'm deceitful, ugly, and a sinner. I'm also foolish and ignorant, thinking I could hide something from the Almighty God.  
Then, I prayed to God and repented. I decided to proactively report this email to my company. As for whether the extorting hacker would publicize the disgraceful videos and how my colleagues would view me after that, I would leave it up to God. God is omnipotent, both just and loving. He accepts and forgives me far beyond what people think and judge; "Let it be what it is."

Immediately, I contacted the CTO, screenshotting the hacker's email in full and asking him to check the status of my hacked email. Later, he arranged for the IT team to investigate the matter.

Although I was no longer worried about the outcome, I still spent a week feeling anxious. In the end, I didn’t receive any further emails from this hacker (they probably also used a mass send, waiting for someone to bite).

It’s been a year since this happened, and I wanted to document it today. 

People often say, "There are gods above us" and "People are doing things, and heaven is watching," but how many really believe there are deities? If there were genuine **faith** in the **true God**, there would be far fewer unjust and unlawful things happening.

For me, as a Christian, I genuinely fear and reverence this omniscient, omnipotent, holy, and just God. In front of Him, I have nowhere to hide, nowhere to run, not just in this life but for eternity. However, my ignorance, limited understanding, and sin still leave me not enough in fear and reverence of this God.
