On February 20, 2019, I received an email "from myself," meaning both the sender and recipient were my work email at Liulishuo, diff@liulishuo.com (so I couldn't see the source of the email). After a quick glance, my initial judgment was: **someone is blackmailing me**.

The gist of the email was:

- Your email has been hacked. Changing the password is meaningless, as the account is completely controlled;
- You have been **browsing pornographic videos online**, and it has been recorded. On one side, your browsing content was recorded, and on the other, who you are and your expressions and behavior while watching, were captured via controlling the front camera;
- Pay **$1000 in Bitcoin as a "hush fee"** to avert disaster;
- If you don't pay within 48 hours, the video recording of your porn browsing will be mass-sent to coworkers, family, and friends in your email contacts.

How did I feel upon receiving this email? 

Firstly, I cannot deny that about two years ago, I watched some pornographic movies. In the last two years, I haven't watched any, but now and then I have seen some scattered pictures of beautiful women, inadvertently or deliberately.

So, I was really scared. **What if the "hackers" mass-send indecent videos of me visiting porn sites to my colleagues and partners? How will they see me?** Perhaps to colleagues and friends without faith, this wouldn't be too serious; they might just laugh it off. What I feared was how they would view this usually devout Christian being so disreputable? How could I face people afterward? (I thought of various domestic and foreign examples of Christians and pastors falling due to pornography and promiscuity.)
What to do?

My immediate reaction was to pay the money to avert disaster, to buy a hush fee. $1000 is not a big number compared to "reputation," but then I quickly realized that this kind of extortion is actually a chain reaction; if you pay the first amount, you'll expect to pay a second. But then again, what if this person really only wants one hush fee? Torn...

When I was anxious, the story of David's sin in the Bible came to my mind. Then, some questions popped up: **Are you a Christian? Do you believe that God is almighty, omnipresent, and omniscient? You fear the embarrassment of things being known to outsiders, don't you fear God knowing these things? Actually, God already knows. You might hide your disgrace from others, but can you hide it from God?**

Faced with these questions, my initial fear of public exposure slowly faded, replaced by shame and sadness. I realized I was someone who did not recognize God, a deceitful and ugly person, a sinner; also a foolish and ignorant person who dared to think of hiding something from an almighty God. 
Then I prayed to God, repented. I decided to proactively report this email to the company. As for whether the blackmailing hacker would ever release the disgraceful videos, and how my colleagues would view me if they did, I left it all to God. God is almighty, both just and loving; His acceptance and forgiveness exceed human evaluation and judgment, "let it be".

Immediately, I contacted the CTO, sent him screenshots of the hacker's email in full, and asked him to check the situation of my mailbox being hacked. Later, he arranged for the IT department colleagues to conduct a related investigation.

Although I no longer worried about the outcome of this matter, I still spent a week feeling anxious. Later, I didn't receive any follow-up emails from the hacker (they were probably performing a system-wide mass-sending, waiting for a response).

A year has passed since this happened, and I'm taking note of it today.

People often say "Three feet above the head, there is a god" and "Heaven watches what people do," but how many truly believe there is a god? If there were true belief in the **true God**, there would be much less injustice, unrighteousness, and illegality.

As a Christian, regarding the omniscient, omnipotent, holy and just God, I truly hold fear and reverence. Before Him, I have nowhere to hide or escape, not only in this life but forever. Yet, because of my foolishness, ignorance, limitations, and sins, my fear and reverence for this God still fall short.
