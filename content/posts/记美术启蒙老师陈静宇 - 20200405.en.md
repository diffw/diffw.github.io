My impression of Teacher Chen Jingyu, drawn with Procreate

I grew up in a small village in Changsha. My parents were farmers and didn’t know how to draw, so they naturally had no influence on my love for or study of art. I remember the first time I drew was by copying cards of the "Saint Seiya" series.

When I started seventh grade, there was a policy that if you enrolled to study art, PE, or music and passed certain levels, you could qualify as a "specialty student" and receive higher points for high school entrance exams. I guess my father wasn't confident about my "bare-handed" exam performance, so I enrolled in the school's art training class. My middle school was called "Jiaotang Middle School," about three or four miles from home, near Changsha Huanghua Airport.

Teacher Chen Jingyu was the school's art teacher and also taught the art specialty student training class. Looking back, she was my art enlightenment teacher.

I was twelve when I started seventh grade. Now looking back, Teacher Chen was probably only 24 years old at that time. I heard she had graduated from Hengyang Teachers' School and had only been working for two years. She was naturally very young, with fair skin, a baby face, big eyes, full lips, and long hair. She dressed subtly and had a unique scent of oil paint, turpentine, and perfume. This aura was completely different from my math teacher Mr. Cao Wenge, physics teacher Mr. Chen Yongquan, and the biology and PE teachers whose names I can't recall. She was an artist.

In art, there's always someone better out there. I believe that although my middle school art teacher, Teacher Chen Jingyu, wasn't the best, at the time, she seemed professional, excellent, and unique. Her skills might not have been the finest, but her pure love for art and painting was rare among the teachers I've known. In class, she was very strict and sometimes spoke harshly; outside class, she invited us to her room to look at art books and paintings in a relaxed and pleasant atmosphere.

Eventually, I did get accepted as a specialty art student, earning 40 extra points on my exams, which, combined with my decent academic performance, exceeded the admission score by several tens of points. After graduating from middle school, I wrote one or two letters to Teacher Chen. Later, she was transferred to a better elementary school. Gradually, we lost contact, and I haven't seen her since.

I'm very grateful to have met Teacher Chen Jingyu when I first started learning art. A good teacher's influence lasts a lifetime. I often think of her and hope to meet her again someday. (If any friends know how to contact Teacher Chen Jingyu, please let me know, thank you.)

Käthe Kollwitz Self Portrait

There's one thing worth mentioning. One day, Teacher Chen handed me an album and said, "You might want to look at the works of Käthe Kollwitz, and maybe try to copy them." When I looked at them, I thought they were quite ugly, but since the teacher recommended them, I gave them a look and made some naive, clumsy copies. It was only many years later that I slowly came to appreciate the beauty of Käthe Kollwitz's works.

During my middle school painting lessons, another thing always comes to mind. After each weekend art class ended, I would sneakingly eat the fruits that were used as drawing props—apples, mandarins, bananas. Those fruits, after being on display for a week, smelled so fragrant and tasted so sweet that I'll always remember them.
