Feb 18, 2021 at 6:38 AM

Get up! Let us go. (Matthew 26:46 NIV)

Jesus was about to suffer. His closest disciples, who should have stayed awake in spirit and body to pray with Jesus during this important and sacred moment, instead fell asleep due to sheer exhaustion.

If I were among the disciples at that time, I would have spent my entire life feeling the deepest regret, despair, and an unforgivable sense of guilt over "falling asleep in the Garden of Gethsemane."

In fact, there's no need to be in the Garden of Gethsemane to experience such despair in our own lives.

The promises made to God during fervent prayer are not fulfilled; the convictions and inspirations repeatedly nudged by the Holy Spirit are ignored or left incomplete. Knowing fully well what is good and righteous, yet failing to follow through...

"What should be done is left undone," first leads me to self-blame, resenting my own weakness and incompetence. Then, it becomes self-condemnation—I fail to keep my promises before God and fall short of His expectations.

Thus, I bind myself with despair, trapped within it. Initially too ashamed, too powerless to turn to God, over time, my heart grows hard, and I end up avoiding God and drifting away. This is truly Satan’s deceit.

Thank Jesus. When the disciples fell into despair, He said, "Get up! Let us go."

His words and grace allowed the disciples to break free from the shame and despair of "falling asleep," and I believe it also liberates me from despair.

As Brother Chambers said, never let the sense of failure corrupt your new action. Never let the sense of failure corrupt your new action.
