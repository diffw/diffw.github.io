Jan 20, 2021 at 6:03 AM

Jesus replied, "Very truly I tell you, no one can see the kingdom of God unless they are born again." (John 3:3)

Obeying God allows me to live in His light (correcting both my inner and outer self), but it doesn't bring about a lively life (freshness). Only by being led by the Holy Spirit can I produce love for God, and thus be willing to listen to God's word and follow it gladly, making life truly vibrant.

I have experienced this "listening and acting" towards God, not because of fear or reverence, but because I feel God's love for me and want to respond to Him. It's very natural. However, such experiences are too few, and I need more of them.

I am certain that I am a reborn person. A reborn person should have a new perspective on everything before being reborn: environment, events, people. Things won't change, but my viewpoint should be refreshed.

I have a question about rebirth. Is rebirth about salvation, or sanctification? If it's about salvation, it should be a one-time occurrence, not something that happens every day. I believe rebirth is about salvation. If it's about salvation, then what is reborn is our "identity"; from sinner, reborn as "considered righteous by God," no longer belonging to the world or the flesh, but to God’s kingdom and the Holy Spirit.

Therefore, I should view everything in life with my reborn "new identity" today. Yet, despite being reborn, sin still entangles me, creating confusion and obscuring my vision as a "new person." I think that only by putting off the old self today and putting on the Lord Jesus can I see clearly.

Gratitude:

1. I'm grateful for hearing YJ's sharing at WD's book club, enhancing my understanding of "sinner." A "sinner" is not defined or judged by human standards, but by God’s standards. Therefore, no one can say one person is better than another in terms of "sin" with their own perspective or preferences. Everyone is equal in that regard, erasing biases against people (even if some are indeed like thorns. Who judges them?)
2. I'm thankful to my wife for organizing my daily meals, so they're ready at meal times. But sometimes I get upset or judge her (sometimes inwardly) over minor things not to my liking, which I truly need to repent for;
3. I'm grateful for God's protection, as I've managed to persevere with 5:30 AM devotionals for 3 weeks now. Starting my day with God's word is the power source for all-day life and work. I've felt this deeply during this period. Also, I thank XF for suggesting writing three gratitude points daily. Besides giving thanks, it helps me quickly review yesterday's people and events, slowing down my aging. Previously, I could never quite recall what happened the day before:)
