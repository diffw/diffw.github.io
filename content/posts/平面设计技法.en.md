In the past couple of days, I've had a bit of free time, so I decided to organize some of my thoughts on design. While searching for "design," I stumbled upon a segment on graphic design techniques on Baidu Baike, and I found it quite rewarding. These techniques were covered in college, and I even practiced each one during that time.

After working for so many years, I've become accustomed to the commercial approach of "more, faster, better, cheaper." This can lead to superficial and mainstream visual expression, causing me to forget those simplest yet most powerful techniques. Reading this article brought back many memories and reminded me of the nights I spent working on assignments in college, making me feel a bit nostalgic.

You can find the [original article here](http://baike.baidu.com/view/14417.html?wtp=tt), and my organized version is [here](http://docs.google.com/View?id=dfc8jsp4_52gx2x5m8q).

**Graphic Design Terminology:**

Harmony, contrast, symmetry, balance, proportion, focal point, rhythm, cadence

**Graphic Design Elements:**

Conceptual elements, visual elements, functional elements, relational elements

Application of Elements:

Composition of points, lines, and planes, gradient, repetition, similarity, skeleton, emission, uniqueness, contrast, density, texture, space, figure-ground, dispersion, rhythm, division, balance, and various arrangements of basic shapes
