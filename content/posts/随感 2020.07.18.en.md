Everyone tries to make themselves and their children healthier, more knowledgeable, and improve their living conditions. As a result, we spend a lot of time on fitness, diet, learning, and work. Today, for the first time, I visited the Sanlin Sports Center near my home and saw many middle-aged and elderly people exercising. I also saw many parents sending their children to swim, practice taekwondo, play badminton, or fence.

Taking care of one's body is not wrong in itself, but I still feel a deep emptiness inside: No matter how healthy or long-lived a person is, if they do not understand the meaning of life, do not know the Creator, and cannot be free from sin, it is still very regrettable. The same goes for knowledge and wealth. Conversely, even if a person is not very healthy or dies young, knowing God is a great blessing. This principle has become increasingly simple for me. Even though my mind is often confused, I still somehow understand. It's just that putting it into practice is so difficult.

Recently, life outwardly lacks nothing, but inwardly it is wild and chaotic.

Not good.

Proverbs 9:10: The fear of the Lord is the beginning of wisdom, and knowledge of the Holy One is understanding.
