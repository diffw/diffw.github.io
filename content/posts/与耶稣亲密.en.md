Have I been with you for so long, and yet you do not know me? (John 14:9)

These words from Jesus were in response to and a guidance for Philip. Before this, Jesus said, "If you had known me, you would have known my Father also. From now on, you do know him and have seen him." And Philip said, "Lord, show us the Father, and it will be enough for us."

Jesus lived with the disciples for three years, not only teaching them through words and actions but also allowing them to witness the miraculous signs and wonders he performed. Jesus even empowered them to perform miracles. Yet, was this still not enough for a disciple to truly know Jesus? From Philip's perspective, it was not. I think the reason might be that Philip still relied on his own limitations (needing the Father to be revealed) and his own demands (to be satisfied), which hindered him from truly knowing Jesus.

What does it mean to know? It's a mutual relationship. Chambers described the relationship Jesus calls "friendship" using Thoughts, Heart, and Spirit.

People (myself included) are limited—limited in physical ability, thoughts, and understanding; people (myself included) are full of sin. Such a person, such as myself, can seemingly never form or establish a relationship with the almighty, exalted, holy, and glorious Jesus in terms of thought, heart, or spirit. It's like a filthy fugitive trying to befriend a majestic, glorious king.

It is often said that a dog's eyes see people as inferior—similarly, human eyes see God as lesser. To truly know God requires humility before Him, acknowledging one's limitations and sins, asking God to cleanse one's mind and motivations, and seeking guidance from the Holy Spirit for understanding the Bible.

Above all, I know a little about the methods to know God, but my inherent sin and rebellion often hinder me from using these methods to know Jesus. I need the Helper (Comforter), the Holy Spirit's rebuke, reminders, and protection. I am always grateful for the Spirit's help and protection because, in my spiritual valleys, I have no power of my own to save myself.

Knowing Jesus is a lifelong journey. I believe that even at the conclusion of one's life, it's impossible to fully know Jesus. However, as Chambers stated, when a disciple is willing to make an effort to pursue a closer relationship with Jesus, he finds joy. In my experience, this is indeed true. Almost every time I open the Bible to read, I find joy; every time my body, initially turned away from God, shifts even one degree towards God at the prompting of the Holy Spirit (just one degree), I find joy. For me, this is a miracle.
