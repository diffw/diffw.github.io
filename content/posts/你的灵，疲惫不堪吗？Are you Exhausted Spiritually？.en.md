Feb 9, 2021 at 6:22 AM

Don't you know? Haven't you heard? The everlasting God, the Lord, the Creator of the ends of the earth, does not grow tired or weary, and his understanding no one can fathom. (Isaiah 40:28 NIV)

Brother Chambers said that exhaustion is the result of depleted vitality. Spiritual exhaustion does not come from sin, but from service.

I often feel exhausted. Although it seems like I am not participating in any "service," upon reflection, maintaining my marriage with my wife, raising and guiding our children, my design work, and playing the piano in church are all my areas of service. In these services, I often feel I'm depleting my love, passion, spirit, and energy.

In theory, as I am connected with Christ, God's love, wisdom, and power should be continuously supplied to me. Since I am constantly filled with God's power, I should be like a "perpetual motion machine," knowing no fatigue.

Slightly differing from Brother Chambers, I believe spiritual exhaustion also comes from sin, even during "service" work. The inability to be a spiritual "perpetual motion machine" is due to the "friction" of sin. Or perhaps sin loosens the pipeline connecting me with Christ, or the pipe has completely fallen off.

The conclusion aligns with Brother Chambers: when I feel exhausted, I should rethink—the original intention of service, the motivation, and the source of strength. Is it from personal compassion, human passion, or based on Christ's redemption?

Now, I slightly understand the meaning of "apart from me, you can do nothing."

For a long time, I thought that minor things in life, habitual things, or skilled matters could be done without Jesus, without relying on Jesus. For instance: without Jesus, I can still design, eat, and walk.

Indeed, I can do these things by myself. However, without Jesus, these things don't last long, and I feel weary doing them. Without Jesus in design, my thinking isn't open, I lack confidence, and I'm not brave enough (to put it simply, those without faith in their work may "abnormally" worry about others' feelings, be swayed by various chaotic factors, fear unknown factors, worry about being slighted, and defend their dignity in design proposals and discussions...); without Jesus, I can still eat, but it's tasteless and joyless. Everything lacks flavor; without Jesus, I can walk, but I can't find joy or a grateful heart in walking. And so on.

When weary, it's necessary to reconnect with Christ, the head.

He gives strength to the weary and increases the power of the weak. Even youths grow tired and weary, and young men stumble and fall; but those who hope in the Lord will renew their strength. They will soar on wings like eagles; they will run and not grow weary, they will walk and not be faint.

(Isaiah 40:29-31 NIV)
