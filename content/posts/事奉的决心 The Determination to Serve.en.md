Feb 23, 2021 at 7:46 AM

Just as the Son of Man did not come to be served, but to serve, and to give his life as a ransom for many. (Matthew 20:28 NIV)

Learning to serve others like Jesus is not easy. With a single misstep, or quite often, we find ourselves wounded by those we serve, leading to a loss of passion and motivation.

Chambers said that Paul's service wasn't rooted in love for people, but in love for the Lord. If we commit ourselves to working for the happiness of others, we will soon be heartbroken and retreat, for man's ingratitude is fiercer than a dog's.

The dog metaphor is quite vivid. It immediately brought to mind the daily scene of serving two children. When growing children are rebellious, disobedient, or offensively spoken, my wife and I often say that raising kids is no better than raising pets.

If the foundation of service is love for people, even love for our own children, disappointment is inevitable because the weaknesses of others will always let a weak person down (a bit of a mouthful). Therefore, as parents, even in the seemingly instinctual and natural "love for children," I must learn to build the root and foundation of service on love for God.

Yesterday was truly a chaotic day. During morning devotions, I understood every word in both Chinese and English, yet felt no inspiration, and couldn't write notes. I spent an hour in the morning communicating with a friend about a design project. Near noon, while researching a video download feature, I got distracted by a movie. The work then dragged into lunch time, delaying the scheduled reading and Bible study session. In the afternoon, I noticed the dry, cracked skin on my left ankle and felt worried. Later in the afternoon, I saw a friend's message about a major stock market drop, and sure enough, a glance at my account confirmed it. In the evening, in a bad mood, I rushed through our family worship and, due to a minor family issue, refused to lead prayer.

The chaos behind this was my sin. My weakness, my tendency to cut corners at work, my lack of self-control in life, and my inability to manage my emotions...

This morning, I came before God to pray, habitually starting with, "Lord, forgive my sins..." As soon as I said it, I realized, what am I saying?!

I actually don't clearly understand what my sins are; or rather, I don't care about the sins in me, thinking I'm doing alright.

Theoretically, I know about the cost of sin, but I can't clearly recognize it, so I "believe it to be true."

Because I don't understand the cost of sin, I naturally can't grasp how essential, vast, and profound the salvation Jesus offers me is.

Without an emotional understanding of Jesus' salvation, how can I love God?

Without loving God, how can I love others and serve?

Conversely, the determination to serve stems from loving God, a natural result of gratitude. To love God starts with recognizing our own sin, filth, and unworthiness, then seeing the dreadful, irrevocable cost of sin, and finally, realizing how necessary and great Jesus' salvation is.

May the Holy Spirit enlighten me, dispel my foolishness, ignorance, and numbness, "to convict me about sin, righteousness, and judgment" (John 16:8 NIV). Help me to understand God's salvation, to be thankful for God's salvation. And then, to love God and consequently to love others.

Gratitude:  
Yesterday was truly dark, but I still consider what I can be thankful for.

1. Thank God for the slow yet steady recovery of my left hand.  
2. Thank my wife for making bread for the family's breakfast the night before.  
3. Thank God for His protection. After yesterday's challenges, this morning I could come before the Lord to read His word and find restoration; though weeping may endure for a night, joy comes in the morning.
