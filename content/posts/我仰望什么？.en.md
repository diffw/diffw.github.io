Jan 22, 2021 at 6:26 AM

"Turn to me and be saved, all you ends of the earth; for I am God, and there is no other." (Isaiah 45:22 NIV)

It turns out that the method of salvation is so simple—just look up to God, turn to Him. It does not even involve any action (no need for ascetic practices), just a change and renewal of the "heart."

However, the change in my heart is the most difficult. Whether it is moving from disbelief in God to belief, developing a disgust for the pleasures of past sins and thus repenting, or renewing my perspective and views on the world, I see any tiny but new changes in my heart as a miracle performed by God in me, the work of the Holy Spirit within.

Looking back at the many instances of returning to God from weakness, valleys, and sin, I believe that when a person looks up to God and turns to Him, aside from the work of the Holy Spirit in me—rebuke, comfort, reminders, and urging—I also need to exert my will. Sometimes, my rebellion reaches a level of "stiff-neckedness," and in such states, I believe, "I need to love the Lord your God with all my heart, with all my soul, and with all my mind." With all my heart, all my soul, all my mind. Striving with all my strength is the duty a person owes to God.

Like ordinary people, like the Israelites, in adversity and suffering it is easy to look up to God, turn to God, depend on God. For, in such situations, knowing my own limitations, I cannot rely on myself.

However, as soon as the circumstances improve slightly, that deadly "pride and ignorance" within me emerges, effectively saying, "God, you can step back now. Watch me take over from here." Thus, quietly, I drift away from God and turn to idols (anything other than God: people, abilities, resources, environment).

Sometimes, when my faith is strong, my heart says, "God, please keep me in adversity so that I won't leave you." From the Old Testament to the New Testament, it seems that almost no prophet, king, or people could steadily stand firm and keep looking up to God in "prosperity and blessings." Humanity is indeed weak and limited.

I pray the Lord helps me every day to look up to Him with all my heart, soul, and mind, keeping my "inner self renewed day by day."
