Sometimes I'm gentle and kind;  
Sometimes I'm cunning and evil;

Sometimes, I love carrots;  
Sometimes, I'm fond of little hens;

I want more;  
And give less;

I hope every rabbit is my friend,  
Yet I think of making all rabbits my dinner;

In the fox's den, I call the shots, do evil,  
And sometimes want to be a rabbit again;

My heart turns to God,  
But sometimes Satan binds me;

Longing for redemption,  
Yet often prideful, scorning the heavens;

Very conflicted,  
Probably stay that way,  
Until life's end;  
Until facing the Most High.

A fox-rabbit;  
Small, real.
