Originally, I intended to [share an unformed idea](http://mp.weixin.qq.com/s?__biz=MzAxNjgyODc4OA==&mid=2651665383&idx=1&sn=ce0c46b8071d89819479b01136befa89&chksm=801743fcb760caeabfa7eabb35d458fc72032944c63222fbf84b75a888c37bc5ccdd07a8a214&scene=21#wechat_redirect) in the form of a diary entry, but unexpectedly, it was shared by friends. Thank you to my old friends for your attention and support.

As a result, 81 new friends have joined. For a small account that's been around for 4 years and has hovered around 350 followers, this many new friends is quite a big deal. It's a bit intimidating—what should I write in today's diary? Will posting cause me to lose followers? I think I will certainly lose some. After thinking for a while, I've settled on a principle: record the present moment and be authentic. I believe that with sincerity and genuine love, some will choose to stay.

What am I thinking about today? What's the next step forward?

Currently, I don't have any new clients. Although I won't run out of resources, it's crucial to get through this stage quickly. My strategy is: continue creating, continue sharing, and wait for business to come to me.

Regarding continuous creation, on one hand, it's about realizing and refining the backlogged ideas one by one. For instance, the animal shapes below, or "Weibo Quick Impressions" that haven't been updated in years (does anyone still remember Weibo Quick Impressions? 😄), and creating some new things. On the other hand, for some interesting projects with potential or friends’ projects, I can offer discounted (or even free) services. In short, don't idle.

![](http://diff.im/blog/wp-content/uploads/2020/03/monster.gif)

Works from around 2015; I should create a series.

As for continuous sharing, what to share? I've been torn about this. My design abilities and design management skills are not considered top-notch in the industry; it seems I don't have much to share. But facing myself honestly and objectively (without inferiority or arrogance), when I look back, I find my thinking, methods, and experiences are quite unique. These hold value for the growth of the new generation of designers. Jokingly speaking, how many designers are there who, having participated in the early startup of a company, used design and led a design team to help the company grow all the way to its IPO? 😊

One thing I've realized is that rhythm is very important. When there's work, you need rhythm to avoid being too busy; when there's no work, you need rhythm to prevent losing direction. I want to find my rhythm through a strict schedule and related time management tools.

Here’s the schedule I've set up, and I’ve stuck to it for two days now. It requires deliberate practice.

**Morning**

- 6:00 - 6:30 Walk/Run
- 6:30 - 7:00 Couple's Worship (Hymns/Bible Reading)
- 7:10 - 8:00 Breakfast (Home/On the Way)

**Forenoon**

- 8:00 - 9:00 Reading: Faith-Related
- 9:00 - 12:00 Work

**Midday**

- 12:00 - 13:30 Lunch/Rest

**Afternoon**

- 13:30-14:30 Learning: Financial/Investment Related
- 14:30-15:30 Work
- 15:30-16:00 Outdoor Activities (With Children)
- 16:00-18:00 Work

**Evening**

- 18:00 - 19:00 Dinner
- 19:00 -20:00 Family Time (Reading, Board Games, Chatting, Drawing, etc.)
- 20:00-20:30 Couples’ Bible Reading (While Putting Children to Sleep)
- 20:30 - 21:00 Reading
- 21:00 -22:30 Write Diary, Wash & Organize, Sleep
