For mothers, there are countless things to worry about once the kids start going to school. But if anything can be called "chaotic," it has to be the morning school run.

From getting the kids out of bed, dressed, washed, fed, to packing their bags and heading out the door, this whole series of tasks must be completed with precision in the brief span of thirty to forty minutes each morning.

However, **innocent and adorable as they are, children are far from being assembly line workers**; they always manage to throw a wrench in the works.

Whether it's fussing about not wanting to get up, buttoning their clothes incorrectly and insisting on doing it all over again, or accidentally spilling a whole bowl of soup down their front... **If insurance companies offered "Child Morning Mishap Insurance," I'm sure every mom would take out their formula money without hesitation to buy it.**

For moms, morning nerves are on edge. From the moment they open their eyes, there's a clock ticking "tick-tock" in their heads. Oh, no, that's not right, it's not a clock, it's a "mishap time bomb." 
So, moms, it's entirely normal to lose your temper a few times in the morning. Otherwise, it would mean the kids are maturing too quickly.

But as a husband, I really don't like living in a heavy, tense family atmosphere early in the morning. Where has the beautiful time of peace, patient love, and mutual affection gone? Moreover, sometimes, in the midst of this morning tension, it's easy for a couple to snap at each other, bust out some frustration or warm up over breakfast.

Since I couldn't stand it any longer, **if I say I can handle it, then I'll do it**.

I told the kids' mom my plan: on Monday, Wednesday, and Friday mornings, **I'll take complete charge of everything from getting the kids up to sending them off to school** (we have two kids, our ten-year-old daughter is fairly independent, but our five-year-old son needs more attention and supervision). Once you're up, stay in the room, absolutely no coming out or helping. Spend your time reading the Bible, meditating, or doing whatever you like. On Tuesday and Thursday, you handle everything, and I'll stay in the room.

The kids' mom gladly accepted.

We tried it out for a week last week, and it worked very well. Mornings became much quieter at home, at least on the days I handled the routine 😄. Of course, the days mom took charge also turned out to be quieter. Let me share how I, as a dad, manage the morning school prep for the kids. At the end, I'll share some thoughts.

### Breakfast for Two Kids

![Representative breakfast, packed, eaten in the car.](http://diff.im/blog/wp-content/uploads/2020/09/IMG_5234-1024x1024.jpeg)

- **6:00** I get up and wash up.
- **6:10** Prepare and pack breakfast. Mainly, it's bread or buns or noodles + egg/steak/beef/ham/chicken + fruit + milk. Staples and meat are prepped in advance and heated in the steamer in the morning. Fruit is handled on the spot. So, it takes about 15 minutes to get everything ready.
- **6:35** Wake up the kids. Allow a 5-minute buffer, aiming to be up by 6:40;
- **6:40** Kids wash up and get dressed;
- **6:50** Head out with backpacks. Note that from a young age, I've required the kids to **organize their backpacks every night** so they can leave the house as soon as they're ready the next day.
- **7:00 - 7:30/35** Drive to school (15 km away), **kids eat breakfast in the car**.
- **7:40** Return home. Start work around 9:00 AM;

The idea behind this "one takes full charge of school mornings" method was primarily to coordinate a few uninterrupted morning times during the week for my wife to read the Bible and meditate, and also for me to have one or two complete morning sessions. When both of us are caring for the kids in the morning, it's not only a waste of human resources but can also lead to conflicts. Secondly, I wanted to put myself through a "stress test" to see if I could handle the chaotic state. To be honest, it's really hard, tense, and challenging. As a result, I have grown to understand my wife better.
