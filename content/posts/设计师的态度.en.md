I'm about to leave my current startup team, and I've promised to help the team complete some designs by the 15th of this month. In reality, once you have the mindset of leaving, it seems like you no longer put your heart into things.

Last night, walking on my way home, I pondered: why is this the case? Shouldn't a true designer take every project at hand seriously? No matter the size, as long as it's still in our hands.

Reflecting on the past, many "trash designs" I'm ashamed to reveal left my hands due to my irresponsibility towards design and a lack of attitude.

I don't know where I'll work next, maybe at a professional design agency, a large corporation, or a startup team, but I hope to always maintain the designer's attitude when facing any design I participate in.

No matter how outstanding design skills and methods are, without a designer's attitude, there will be no achievement.
