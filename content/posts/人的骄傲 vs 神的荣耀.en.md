I am a person who is both insecure and proud. Years ago, I was astonished to recognize these seemingly opposite traits coexisting within me. At that time, I was already a follower of Jesus. Upon further reflection, I realized that insecurity masked pride, and conversely, pride concealed insecurity. In essence, both are pride.

>Pride goes before destruction, a haughty spirit before a fall. (Proverbs 16:18 NIV)

The pride in my heart is immense, eager to seize every opportunity to showcase myself at any time, in any place, to prove my ability and worth, seeking others' recognition, admiration, and praise. This is the corruption and sin in my heart.

Whenever I decide to do something, even if it's meaningful and beneficial to others, I find it incredibly easy to fall into "fulfilling my own pride," fantasizing about the glory and success to follow. At this point, I am subtly controlled by evil without realizing it.

Following "fulfilling my own pride" brings worry, fear, hesitation, and weakness. Because after imagining the "glory of success," concerns about "what if I fail?" naturally arise. How will others evaluate me if I fail? Will they laugh at me? Will they belittle me? Slowly, I lose courage, start to belittle myself, doubt myself—am I capable enough? Thus, the once beautiful idea, possibly a grand plan or project, ends prematurely, undone by my human pride.

The opposite motive is: For the glory of God.

I believe that by myself, I cannot generate the motivation "for the glory of God." Every time the motive to fulfill my own pride arises, a small yet powerful "voice" reminds me: for the glory of God (though, I hesitate to use the word "voice" as it may cause misunderstanding). After experiencing this many times, I realized this reminder does not originate from my own thoughts. I believe it is the Holy Spirit dwelling within me who reminds me.

When I choose to turn to "doing things for the glory of God" (even though I do not always have the strength to choose this way), all the worry, fear, hesitation, and weakness that come from "achieving my own pride" vanish; replaced by hope, courage, determination, and strength. Thus, I feel as if I am uplifted from an invisible shackle, becoming instantly relaxed and free.

I no longer need to prove my worth by accomplishing something; my worth is in God's hands. Even if I don't do this thing, my value in God's eyes remains unchanged; I see my abilities accurately. God knows my abilities; God is aware of my deficiencies. I ask God to use me as such an unworthy vessel, granting me the wisdom, ability, and strength needed to accomplish His will; I no longer make my benefit or interest the purpose of my actions. What I hope for is that this endeavor can help people and edify them, even if it's just a little help, benefiting only a few.

>In the same way, let your light shine before others, that they may see your good deeds and glorify your Father in heaven. (Matthew 5:16)
