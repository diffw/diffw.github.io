So, we were therefore buried with him through baptism into death in order that, just as Christ was raised from the dead through the glory of the Father, we too may live a new life. (Romans 6:4)

Before believing in Jesus, I was a person who made decisions for myself, seemingly in control of everything.

After believing in Jesus, I began to somewhat understand God's words such as "baptized into his death," "take up your cross and follow me," "I no longer live, but Christ lives in me," etc., which indicate and tell me to hand over the sovereignty of my life (which, essentially, I do not own or control) to Jesus.

This process of transferring sovereignty is extremely difficult and arduous. It's like a defeated general kneeling before the victor, handing over the command seal, and doing so willingly and wholeheartedly.

Looking back, I've had several experiences of total surrender before God, admitting that I am powerless and have nothing. In those moments, I have tasted the "new life," experiencing the eternal sense of "righteousness, peace, and joy in the Holy Spirit."

However, my inner folly, pride, and rebellion have repeatedly launched "coups," attempting to seize sovereignty that was never mine to begin with. Thus, I fall into life's troughs time after time.

It's truly miraculous logic that giving up sovereignty means victory. And victory is found in the Bible, in peace, and in righteousness.
