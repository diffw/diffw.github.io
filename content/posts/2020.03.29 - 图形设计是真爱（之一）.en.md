My major in college was graphic design. However, when I first enrolled, the official name was "Graphic Decoration." That was in 2001, and the term "Visual Communication" only started gaining popularity in China around 2004.

In the last semester of my senior year in high school, the school needed a new logo. My art teacher, Mr. Chen Wenzhi, initiated a contest among all the art students (although it was more like a practice exercise). As a result, this group of future "design stars" couldn't come up with anything beyond a design featuring an "open book" graphic (just like the shape of this book emoji 📖), which was quite clichéd. At that time, Mr. Chen gave us a basic introduction to logo design.

During university, I became deeply fascinated with positive and negative space graphics: simplicity and ingenuity. The first time I saw positive and negative space designs, I was amazed at how they worked.

Gradually, my fascination with graphics went deep into my bones.  
I believe every unique graphic is a brand new creation. People fight over domain names and trademark words, but simple, excellent graphics are also incredibly valuable assets. For example: the cross, BMW, Mercedes-Benz, Apple, Nike.

In the summer of 2002, influenced by my brother and a friend named Gui, I was introduced to FrontPage, a web design tool. Then came the "Dreamweaver Trio." Beyond graphics, web pages and animation became my new hobbies. Over time, this hobby allowed me to support myself at school. Later, I worked in web design, gradually moving away from jobs in advertising or brand design companies related to graphics. In 2009, I got into UI design. The interaction with touchscreens was an evolution of my love for web interaction. Besides passion, there was also the practical benefit—UI design pays well. Concepts like user experience and interaction design came a bit later.

Recently, I worked on a brand design project for a friend. As I focused again on graphics, I experienced a state of flow: immersion with both challenge and confidence. I enjoyed the process of creativity, crafting, and attention to detail. Putting aside external material entanglements, I re-evaluated my inner preferences, and I must say, graphic design is true love. This is part one. 😄
