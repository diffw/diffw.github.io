A friend suggested that the title shouldn't be too plain. This time, it's eye-catching enough, right?

[I'm very passionate about creating with graphics and colors](http://mp.weixin.qq.com/s?__biz=MzAxNjgyODc4OA==&mid=2651665421&idx=1&sn=04e498520e5cd1146113954fd54f76fc&chksm=80174416b760cd005bbc21872085c33ef7c9201ecf79f506737caba1458352dafd02d22a751d&scene=21#wechat_redirect). Yet, at different stages, I always feel my understanding of these two areas is lacking, hence the need for periodic practice. These are exercises I've been doing recently.

Creating one piece a day is quite a fast pace. After dinner or breakfast, I lean back on the sofa for 15-20 minutes, briefly reflecting on the day's experiences or thoughts. I choose a few color combinations (this takes some time) and start creating freely, without worrying about the precision of the work.

It's quite a fun process, especially when accumulated over time, the works start to have a series vibe. By playing like this, I keep my sensitivity to graphics and colors, while simultaneously gathering material for future design and creation. Isn't that great?

Suddenly, I had the idea to create a "Designer Daily Practice" group, so people with the same mindset can come together for some amateur creativity, not so lonely. If you're interested in joining, leave a comment and raise your hand. We'll form the group if we get over 10 people.

Additionally, here's some surprising news: this account has been running for 4 years, published 87 pieces (images/text), and the day before yesterday, we finally surpassed 500 followers. 😄
