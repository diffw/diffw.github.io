When I flipped through my journal from last August, I saw a version of myself that hasn't grown in a year—no progress in my faith, no progress in life's direction.

In faith, I still linger on the outskirts. Compared to last year, this year's state seems worse because I've become numb. Regardless of the testimonies of fellow believers, the sermons, or the sharing, I no longer feel moved; it has become routine. I deceive myself, feeling superficially satisfied without being alert, thinking all is well. To others, I appear to be a person of faith, often talking about God, seeming different from others to be considered a serious seeker by brothers and sisters in the church. But if I am honest with myself, am I truly serious? I'm not. I participate in church activities, talk about serving with guitar accompaniment, more for the sake of relationships, my hobbies, and vanity, rather than for God. My heart remains hard, resistant to God. Even with various aspirations to seek God, I cannot take even a small step.

As Yanbing said, I cannot continue like this year after year, not next year, not the year after, not when I am old. What is the meaning of life then?

In prayer, I found myself weeping uncontrollably. I can't understand this emotional release and can't control my emotions. I ask God to forgive my repeated lack of effort in the past and to give me another chance to try again. My personality harbors much pride and self-esteem, often swayed by others’ words into doubting God, weary and skeptical of parts of the Bible I can't understand. I am fixated on material things and reality, blind to God, often unable to control bodily desires and engaging in impure actions. Thinking of these transgressions, knowing they are all under God's watchful eye, fills me with fear, shame, and guilt.

I truly cannot continue like this any longer.

As I write this, there's a commotion downstairs. More and more people are gathering, a woman started crying, and foul language and shouting ensue, both sides accusing each other of being unreasonable.

What is reason? Can reason come from conflict?

How many such disputes have occurred through the ages? They truly are meaningless. After arguing, perhaps they won't see each other again—what meaning does such conflict bring to their lives? Let alone benefits.

I realize faith is truly beautiful.

For this week, I am willing to be serious for once, to seek God. My self-control is very poor, I lack the ability to govern myself, doing things driven by impulse and desire. But this week, I hope to overcome myself. I need God's help to support me when I falter.

I also wish to have well-organized time this week, with a life that's planned, rhythmic, and disciplined.

I also wish for humility at work. Compared to my younger colleagues, I want to maintain humility, not fearing to lose my stance because of it. I wish to act justly, honestly, and truthfully, even if things are not beneficial to me or make my life harder. I pray for submission at work, understanding God's purpose for me each day, and having morning prayers to seek His will.

I'm also entrusting my sub-health and occupational illness symptoms. Truly, I ask for God's healing. For someone like me who lacks faith, I also wish to exercise daily, with gratitude during exercise, thanking God for life and a healthy body, and not becoming prideful because of my strength. I ask for God's healing.

Lastly, life's direction. In prayer, I express everything to God, including all my doubts about Him. I wish to be completely open to God in prayer. In fact, even if I hide things, God knows my thoughts completely. I don't have the faith to ask God to arrange my future direction because I love art and design. I worry that God's will lies outside these areas. I lack faith because I don't know what else I could love or rely on for a living outside these. Without faith. When I looked back at last year's journal, there was one entry thinking about life's direction, surprisingly similar to this current phase, almost identical. I find myself laughable for still being in the same place after a year. Actually, it's not just a year—it's been three. My life's direction has seen neither action nor planning advancement. After several years, it remains almost unchanged. I don't know if I would set similarly unprogressive plans next year without tonight's realization.

I'm an ordinary intellect person with no courage, strength, persistence, seeking pleasure. What can I do? Can I accomplish my life's goals? Or am I destined to make superficial plans each year and live within them perpetually?

Because I lack faith, I can't instill confidence in myself. My surroundings and environment not only fail to provide confidence, reality makes me feel ashamed, feeling incapable compared to others. As I lack the foundational conditions to achieve dreams.

But even if I'm successful in the world, achieving self-imposed life goals—what's the point? Ultimately, one cannot escape life's decline! Besides, worldly success, beyond material enjoyment, and so-called spiritual enjoyment based on material foundation, what else is there? I can't guarantee it brings more joy and happiness than my current state within God.

I want to listen to God's will. Because of my little faith, I question God in prayer: if when I am old, I discover my faith in God is a void, wouldn't my life be tragic, sorrowful? I ask God for forgiveness for my irreverence and offense. I have these thoughts because of outside voices challenging my faith, causing such disturbance. May God help me by removing all disturbances.

After these prayers, I felt a call: Should I attend this week's Bible study? Immediately I felt an inner conflict. One side says, "Yes, attend, take this step;" another side says, "Next week you need time to keep practicing guitar, or think about life's direction, or paint."

Regarding guitar practice, in prayer I declared my guitar will be played only for God. Recently, I have been practicing popular songs, but now I think, henceforth, I'll only play hymns.

The quest for life's direction has been ongoing for years with no change or progress; let it go. Since it has been entrusted to God in prayer, why should this week be like all others, relying on myself alone? What I most need now is to take a step forward, cultivating genuine dedication in faith, returning to God.

I grit my teeth and decide to attend the Bible study this week. I also pray for God to prepare everything and remove all distractions regarding this matter. Attending Bible study, solely for knowing God, removing all thoughts related to people.

Thank God. Thankful for the guidance, correction, and assistance of brothers and sisters in Christ. I pray for God's guidance and use in our lives.
