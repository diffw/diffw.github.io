Arise, shine! (Isaiah 60:1 NIV)

Thanks to God's mercy and grace, and through Jesus Christ, I have already left the darkness and walk in the light. God's redemption has freed me from sin and death, and from being a sinner, God has counted me as righteous. The radiant light of God has changed, and is continually changing, my life.

Having received God's grace, I should glorify God in my life.

To glorify God is to naturally express the glory of God, which has changed and renewed my life, through my transformed words and actions; it is to live out a new life where "it is no longer I who live, but Christ lives in me" in the presence of those around me, before family, friends, and colleagues.

When Moses descended Mount Sinai with the two tablets of the law in his hands, he was unaware that his face was radiant because he had spoken with the Lord. Aaron and all the Israelites saw Moses' radiant face and were afraid to come near him. (Exodus 34:29-30 NIV)

To shine is not to emit light of one's own, for there is no light in me; rather, it is God's light illuminating me and being reflected; shining is not for my own benefit since I am already a beneficiary of God's light.

To shine, revealing the glorious light of God, is a sacred act; however, the actions initiated often involve ordinary, trivial, tedious, seemingly worthless, and humble tasks.

The same task—a mundane, small, tedious, humble endeavor, when done by someone illuminated by God's glory, by someone willing to shine for the Lord—yields completely different results, benefits, and impacts on others. This "difference" is shining.

Lord, I ask that you renew my perspective and spirit, so that I see everyday, small tasks and my daily routine as opportunities to arise and shine for you. For the Lord Jesus is my example. To manifest the Father's glory and redeem me (and mankind), he left the glorious throne of heaven, humbling himself to become human flesh. This is the most humble and humiliating work, yet also the greatest and most glorious.
